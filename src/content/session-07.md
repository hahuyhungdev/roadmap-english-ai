# BUỔI 7: Describe a Problem + Root Cause

**Level:** B1+ / B2  
**Focus:** Speak clearly and analytically about a specific problem, identify root causes, and discuss its consequences using precise vocabulary and logical structure.

## 1) Vocabulary (Expanded & Sophisticated for B2)

- **root cause** (n) – the fundamental reason behind a problem
  - Identifying the root cause is more important than fixing surface symptoms.

- **bottleneck** (n) – a point that restricts flow or progress
  - The slow API response created a major bottleneck in the user journey.

- **symptom** (n) – a visible sign of an underlying problem
  - Long loading times were just a symptom of deeper performance issues.

- **impact** (n) – the effect or influence (often negative)
  - The issue had a significant impact on user satisfaction and conversion rates.

- **friction** (n) – difficulty or resistance experienced by users
  - Excessive form fields created unnecessary friction during checkout.

- **scalability issue** (n) – a problem that appears when the system grows
  - We encountered scalability issues as the number of concurrent users increased.

- **regression** (n) – a problem that reappears after a change
  - A recent update introduced a regression in the mobile navigation menu.

- **constraint** (n) – a limitation or restriction
  - Browser memory constraints limited how many components we could render at once.

- **cascade effect** (n) – when one problem triggers multiple others
  - The initial delay caused a cascade effect across the entire dashboard.

## 2) Collocations, Chunking & Advanced Phrases

### Strong Collocations

- The problem I want to describe is…
- On the surface, it looked like… but actually…
- After investigating, I discovered that the root cause was…
- This issue stemmed from…
- As a result, users experienced…
- The consequences went beyond…
- From a business perspective, this led to…
- What made the situation worse was…

### Advanced Phrasal Verbs

- **stem from** → The delay stemmed from inefficient data fetching.
- **break down** → I had to break down the problem step by step.
- **dig into** → We dug into the logs to find the real cause.
- **snowball into** → A small rendering issue can snowball into a major performance problem.
- **account for** → We failed to account for high traffic spikes.

## 3) Typical Dialogues

### Dialogue 1 – Full Problem + Root Cause Explanation (3–4 minutes)

**Teacher:** Tell me about a problem you faced recently and its root cause.

**You:**  
One problem we encountered was that the main dashboard was loading very slowly, sometimes taking more than 8 seconds. On the surface, it seemed like a simple performance issue, but after digging into it, I found the root cause was much deeper.

The main culprit was that we were fetching large amounts of unnecessary data on every page load without proper pagination or caching. Additionally, some components were re-rendering excessively because state management wasn’t optimized. These two factors combined created a cascade effect, especially during peak hours when many users accessed the system simultaneously.

As a result, users became frustrated, productivity dropped, and we even saw a measurable decline in task completion rates. From a business perspective, this kind of friction can lead to lower user adoption and increased support tickets.

### Dialogue 2 – Handling Objection / Deeper Question

**Teacher:** Was it really that serious?

**You:**  
Yes, because it wasn’t just about speed. The slow dashboard affected decision-making for managers who rely on real-time data. When the system feels unresponsive, users start losing trust, and that can have long-term consequences for the entire platform’s reputation.

## 4) Reading Text

Last month our team faced a noticeable performance degradation on the main analytics dashboard. Users reported that pages were taking too long to load, especially when filtering large datasets. At first glance, it appeared to be a frontend rendering problem. However, after careful investigation, we identified the root causes: inefficient API calls that returned excessive data, lack of memoization in heavy components, and no caching strategy for repeated queries.

These issues created significant user friction and led to a cascade effect during busy periods. The business impact was clear — reduced productivity, more support requests, and a temporary drop in user satisfaction scores. Recognizing the root causes allowed us to prioritize the right solutions instead of treating only the symptoms.

## 5) List of Questions + Ideas

### Basic to Intermediate

1. Can you describe a recent problem you faced in your project?
2. What was the most visible symptom of the problem?
3. What do you think was the root cause?
4. How did the problem affect users?

### B2-Level Expanded Questions

5. Walk me through how you identified the root cause.
6. What kind of cascade effects did the problem create?
7. How did this issue impact the business or team productivity?
8. Was the problem related to scalability, compatibility, or something else?
9. What constraints made the problem more difficult to solve?
10. If the same problem occurred again, what would you do differently?

### Discussion & Critical Thinking

11. Why is it important to distinguish between symptoms and root causes?
12. How do you explain a technical problem and its impact to non-technical stakeholders?
13. What makes a problem “serious” in a frontend development context?
14. How can small technical decisions lead to big user experience issues?
15. Describe a problem that seemed simple at first but turned out to be complex.
