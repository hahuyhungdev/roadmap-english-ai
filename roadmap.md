# English for IT & Business — Full Curriculum (Updated)

> **Target:** Interview-ready English for foreign tech companies
> **Format:** 1-on-1 with teacher + self-study
> **Total:** 26 sessions + 2 mock interviews

---

## 🧩 PHASE 1 — TECH & BUSINESS

### Sessions 01–05
- **Status:** Keep unchanged
- **Note:** No update by request

### Session 06 — Documentation as a Product
- **Focus:** Docs create value: onboarding, reducing support load, and team alignment.
- **Scope:** Talk about runbooks, API docs, handover notes, and how documentation improves team speed with clear metrics.

### Session 07 — Project Walkthrough (NEW)
- **Focus:** Explain one project end-to-end: problem -> system -> role -> impact.
- **Interview link:** Build your core "Tell me about a project" story (can also support "Tell me about yourself" with a project-first structure).
- **Scope:** Cover one real project only: business problem, system flow, your ownership, trade-off, measurable result, and lesson learned.

---

## 🔥 PHASE 2 — ANALYTICAL THINKING IN IT (Keep Core)

### Session 08 — Root Cause Analysis & Impact
- **Focus:** Structured debugging + impact quantification.
- **Scope:** Explain incident investigation steps (logs, timeline, root cause) and quantify impact with numbers like downtime, users affected, and business loss.

### Session 09 — Incident Under Pressure
- **Focus:** Handling outages + clear communication under stress.
- **Scope:** Talk about real-time response: role in outage, coordination, status updates, escalation, rollback, and post-incident follow-up.

### Session 10 — Defending Decisions
- **Focus:** Influence without authority.
- **Boundary:** Daily decision quality (scope cut, priority choice, practical compromise) stays here, not only crisis mode.
- **Scope:** Practice how to defend choices with cost/risk/timeline language when PM, lead, or stakeholders disagree.

### Session 11 — Owning Failure
- **Focus:** Accountability + trust rebuilding.
- **Scope:** Talk about one failure story clearly: what happened, your responsibility, immediate fix, prevention action, and how you rebuilt trust.

---

## 🧠 PHASE 3 — PRACTICAL DISCUSSION (Refined, Non-Overlap)

> Each session has one clear axis, no overlap.

### Session 12 — Energy, Burnout & Sustainability
- **Focus:** Manage energy, avoid burnout, realistic productivity.
- **Boundary:** About pace and energy management, not life meaning/identity.
- **Scope:** Discuss signs of overload, workload control, boundaries, rest habits, and what sustainable performance looks like week to week.

### Session 13 — Self-Perception & Confidence
- **Focus:** Imposter syndrome, comparison, self-worth.
- **Scope:** Talk about confidence in real situations: comparing with peers, self-doubt after setbacks, and practical ways to rebuild self-trust.

### Session 14 — Money & Real-Life Trade-offs
- **Focus:** Saving vs spending, lifestyle pressure, financial decisions.
- **Boundary:** Personal life finance only; workplace salary negotiation is in Session 24.
- **Scope:** Discuss budgeting choices, emergency fund, lifestyle inflation, social pressure, and trade-offs in personal money decisions.

### Session 15 — Relationships & Boundaries
- **Focus:** Friends, family, saying no, protecting time.
- **Scope:** Talk about communication in close relationships: setting boundaries, handling expectations, and protecting focus without hurting trust.

### Session 16 — Direction, Meaning & Uncertainty
- **Focus:** Not knowing your path, changing goals, identity shifts.
- **Boundary:** Meaning and identity decisions, not burnout mechanics (Session 12).
- **Scope:** Discuss uncertainty in career/life direction, changing goals over time, and how to make choices when identity is still evolving.

### Session 17 — Learning & Skill Growth
- **Focus:** How people really improve skills long-term.
- **Scope:** Talk about practical skill growth methods: deliberate practice, feedback loop, consistency, and applying learning in real projects.

### Session 18 — Global Work & Communication
- **Focus:** Cross-cultural work, async communication, and clarity.
- **Scope:** Discuss real collaboration across cultures/time zones: communication style gaps, async updates, clarification habits, and trust building.

### Session 19 — Ethics & Real Decisions
- **Focus:** Doing the right thing under pressure (not theory).
- **Scope:** Talk about ethical choices in real work constraints: known risks, pressure to ship, speaking up, and responsible escalation.

---

## 🎭 PHASE 4 — BEHAVIORAL & INTERVIEW (Expanded, Non-Overlap)

### Session 20 — Conflict with Difficult People
- **Focus:** Emotional conflict, toxic situations.
- **Scope:** Practice handling toxic behavior calmly: de-escalation, boundary setting, documentation, and escalation path.

### Session 21 — Ownership & Initiative
- **Focus:** Taking responsibility beyond your role.
- **Scope:** Talk about stepping up without title: spotting gaps, unblocking teams, following through, and driving results proactively.

### Session 22 — Decision Making Under Uncertainty
- **Focus:** Acting without full information.
- **Boundary:** Interview storytelling for uncertainty; technical crisis handling remains in Sessions 08-09.
- **Scope:** Discuss how you decide with incomplete data: assumptions, downside planning, trade-offs, and final decision logic.

### Session 23 — Motivation & Personal Values
- **Focus:** What truly drives you (real, not textbook).
- **Scope:** Talk about personal drivers, value conflicts, meaningful work, and real examples of choices guided by your values.

### Session 24 — Negotiation & Professional Feedback
- **Focus:** Salary negotiation + giving/receiving feedback.
- **Boundary:** Workplace compensation and communication; personal spending habits remain in Session 14.
- **Scope:** Practice salary conversation structure, evidence-based negotiation, and giving/receiving feedback with clear follow-up actions.

---

## Topic Guardrails (Final)

- Session 12 != Session 16: Session 12 is energy/pacing; Session 16 is direction/meaning.
- Session 14 != Session 24: Session 14 is personal finance trade-offs; Session 24 is professional salary/feedback.
- Session 10 != Session 22: Session 10 is influencing and defending choices; Session 22 is deciding with incomplete information.
- Session 08-09 != Session 22: 08-09 are incident-specific; 22 is broader interview decision-making.

## 🎯 PHASE 5 — MOCK INTERVIEW

### Sessions 25–26
- **Focus:** End-to-end mock interviews + gap closing.

---

### Session 25 — Mock Interview 1

Full simulated interview — 15 minutes behavioral (STAR method), 15 minutes discussion (Phase 3 topics), 5 minutes Q&A.
Teacher acts as a professional interviewer: asks follow-up questions, pushes back on weak answers.
Record the session for self-review. Focus on: fluency, structure, business language, and confidence under pressure.
No detailed feedback yet — just self-diagnosis after listening back.
- **Scope:** Full simulation with pressure and follow-up questions; focus on structure, fluency, and content clarity.

---

### Session 26 — Final Mock Interview & Gap Analysis

Second full mock interview incorporating lessons from Mock 1.
Aim for consistent band 7–8 level communication throughout.
After the mock: structured post-mortem — identify top 2 strengths and top 2 remaining gaps.
Finish with a personal improvement roadmap: what to keep practicing, what resources to use, what to do next.
- **Scope:** Final performance check with targeted gap analysis and concrete next-step practice plan.
