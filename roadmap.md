# English for IT & Business - Personalized Curriculum (No-Overlap V3)

> Target: Interview-ready English for foreign tech companies
> Learner profile: 25-27-year-old Vietnamese software engineer (B1-B2)
> Format: 1-on-1 with teacher + self-study
> Total: 26 sessions + 2 mock interviews

---

## PHASE 1 - TECH & BUSINESS FOUNDATION (FRONTEND + GENERAL)

### Phase 1 Practical Knowledge Stack (Applied)

- Component docs and Storybook language: props, states, usage examples, and ownership.
- UI behavior language: loading/empty/error states, responsive behavior, accessibility checks.
- Design handoff language: what is clear in wireframes and what still needs clarification.
- API contract language from frontend view: request/response expectations, error handling, integration risk.
- Prompt workflow language: drafting docs, refining interview answers, and checking quality.
- Cross-functional clarity: turning technical work into PM, QA, designer, and leadership updates.

### Session 01 - Role and Business Value

- Focus: Explain your role in business language with frontend-friendly examples.
- In scope: Ownership, team contribution, measurable impact, user/business value statements.
- Out of scope: Deep architecture explanation.
- Required output: 60-second self-intro linking your role to business outcomes.
- Practical knowledge targets: impact metrics, value statements, role scope boundaries.

### Session 02 - System and Stakeholders

- Focus: Explain system flow and communication with non-engineering partners.
- In scope: User flow, dependency flow, stakeholder expectations, alignment updates, risk visibility.
- Out of scope: Personal career motivation.
- Required output: 1 system walkthrough + 1 stakeholder update message.
- Practical knowledge targets: brief doc summary, wireframe-to-system explanation,
  stakeholder-friendly technical language.

### Session 03 - Tech Stack and Trade-offs

- Focus: Justify technical choices with practical constraints and clear trade-offs.
- In scope: Framework/tool selection, speed vs stability, maintainability, integration risk.
- Out of scope: Legacy debt cleanup planning.
- Required output: 1 trade-off table + 90-second decision explanation.
- Practical knowledge targets: API contract decisions, frontend-platform constraints,
  integration risk language.

### Session 04 - Technical Debt and Change Management

- Focus: Discuss debt honestly and plan safe change.
- In scope: Debt signals, prioritization, incremental refactor, release risk control.
- Out of scope: AI tooling productivity debates.
- Required output: 1 debt-priority list + 1 change rollout script.
- Practical knowledge targets: change brief, migration checklist,
  rollback communication template.

### Session 05 - AI Tools and the Changing Developer Role

- Focus: Use AI tools effectively while protecting engineering judgment.
- In scope: Daily AI usage, validation habits, speed-quality trade-off, skill risk.
- Out of scope: Incident response communication.
- Required output: 1 AI usage policy for personal workflow + 1 interview answer on AI limits.
- Practical knowledge targets: prompt design, context packaging,
  output verification checklist.

### Session 06 - Documentation as a Product

- Focus: Docs as business leverage (faster onboarding, lower QA rework, clearer handoff).
- In scope: Component guidelines, UI state specs, handoff notes, Storybook pages,
  changelog habits, doc ownership, measurable impact.
- Out of scope: Deep ops runbook workflows.
- Required output: 1 docs-impact statement with concrete metrics +
  1 mini doc bundle (component guideline + wireframe note + Storybook/props reference).

### Session 07 - Project Walkthrough

- Focus: Explain one project end-to-end (problem -> flow -> role -> impact).
- In scope: Business problem, system flow, ownership, trade-off, measurable result.
- Out of scope: Multi-project comparison.
- Required output: 2-minute project story + 3 follow-up interviewer answers.
- Practical knowledge targets: connect handoff -> implementation -> QA -> final impact story.

## PHASE 2 - ANALYTICAL THINKING IN IT (FRONTEND-READY, TRANSFERABLE)

### Session 08 - Root Cause Analysis & Impact

- Focus: Structured diagnosis + quantified impact.
- In scope: Timeline, logs/traces, root-cause reasoning, impact numbers.
- Out of scope: Live outage communication.
- Required output: 1 RCA timeline + 1 impact statement with numbers.

### Session 09 - Scaling Systems Under Real Constraints

- Focus: Explain how to scale reliably under budget, timeline, and team limits.
- In scope: Bottleneck identification, capacity planning, cost-performance trade-offs,
  phased scaling, and stakeholder alignment.
- Out of scope: Live outage war-room communication.
- Required output: 1 scaling constraint map + 1 phased scaling plan + 1 90-second interview answer.

### Session 10 - Defending Decisions

- Focus: Persuasion without authority.
- In scope: Cost-risk-timeline arguments, stakeholder pushback handling,
  speed vs quality framing.
- Out of scope: General uncertainty framework.
- Required output: 1 short decision memo + 1 pushback dialogue.

### Session 11 - Owning Failure

- Focus: Accountability and trust recovery.
- In scope: Honest failure narrative, response, prevention, trust rebuild.
- Out of scope: Repeating RCA mechanics from Sessions 08-09.
- Required output: 1 STAR failure answer + 1 prevention plan + 1 trust-rebuild follow-up message.

## PHASE 3 - MODERN SOCIETY TOPICS (PERSONALIZED)

### Session 12 - Mental Health, Burnout & Recovery

- Focus: Energy collapse signals and recovery habits.
- In scope: Overload signs, recovery routines, boundary scripts.
- Out of scope: Identity crisis and life meaning.
- Required output: 7-day energy log + 3 boundary phrases.

### Session 13 - Career Comparison & Imposter Syndrome

- Focus: Handle career comparison pressure without losing direction.
- In scope: Imposter triggers, confidence repair, realistic benchmarks,
  and healthier progress framing.
- Out of scope: Personal branding strategy.
- Required output: 90-second honest answer to "I felt behind my peers" + 1 personal benchmark reset note.

### Session 14 - Fake Identity & Authentic Personal Brand

- Focus: Curated online image vs real capability.
- In scope: LinkedIn/GitHub persona, authenticity, credibility language.
- Out of scope: Salary negotiation scripts.
- Required output: 60-second authentic self-intro + "what I am still learning" line.

### Session 15 - Bullying, Exclusion & Psychological Safety

- Focus: Harmful behavior and safe response.
- In scope: Public shaming, exclusion, evidence, escalation path.
- Out of scope: Normal disagreement between peers.
- Required output: 1 escalation script + 1 incident note template.

### Session 16 - Success Pressure & Family Expectations

- Focus: Pressure from income/status expectations.
- In scope: Fear of failure, social comparison, practical coping choices.
- Out of scope: Workplace feedback mechanics.
- Required output: 1 personal trade-off map (money, health, growth, stability).

### Session 17 - Attention, Distraction & Deep Work

- Focus: Attention economy and focus control.
- In scope: Notification overload, context switching, focus rituals.
- Out of scope: Burnout diagnostics.
- Required output: 1 distraction audit + 1 weekly deep-work plan.

### Session 18 - Human Connection in Remote Work

- Focus: Build real connection and belonging in remote teams.
- In scope: Isolation signals in remote work, trust rituals,
  meaningful check-ins, and team belonging habits.
- Out of scope: Deep async process design.
- Required output: 1 weekly human-connection routine + 1 team check-in script.

### Session 19 - Environmental Responsibility in Software Engineering

- Focus: Balance product delivery with environmental impact.
- In scope: Energy-efficient design choices, cloud/resource usage,
  performance vs carbon trade-offs, and practical green engineering habits.
- Out of scope: Broad climate policy debates without engineering action.
- Required output: 1 engineering sustainability checklist + 1 green trade-off decision note.

## PHASE 4 - BEHAVIORAL & INTERVIEW EXECUTION

### Session 20 - Conflict with Difficult People

- Focus: De-escalation and professional boundaries.
- In scope: Tough conversations with difficult teammates or managers.
- Out of scope: Bullying/harassment case handling from Session 15.
- Required output: 1 difficult-conversation script with follow-up actions.

### Session 21 - Ownership & Initiative

- Focus: Taking responsibility beyond job title.
- In scope: Spotting gaps, proposing actions, delivering outcomes.
- Out of scope: Compensation and negotiation.
- Required output: 1 ownership story with before/after impact.

### Session 22 - Decision Making Under Uncertainty

- Focus: Acting with incomplete information.
- In scope: Assumptions, downside planning, reversible vs irreversible decisions.
- Out of scope: Defending a decision after alignment is already set.
- Required output: 1 uncertainty decision matrix + 1 interview answer.

### Session 23 - Motivation, Values & Long-Term Fit

- Focus: What drives you and where you fit.
- In scope: Value conflicts, role fit, realistic long-term direction.
- Out of scope: Comparison jealousy and fake identity topics.
- Required output: 1 value-priority map + 1 "why this company" answer.

### Session 24 - Negotiation & Professional Feedback

- Focus: Salary discussion + feedback conversations.
- In scope: Evidence-based negotiation, giving/receiving feedback, alignment close.
- Out of scope: Personal finance habits and family pressure.
- Required output: 1 salary script + 1 feedback conversation script.
