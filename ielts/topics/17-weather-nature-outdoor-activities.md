## Part 2 - Vocabulary In Context

### 2a. Word Families

1. **weather / weather / weather-related**
   - Meaning: temperature, rain, wind, sun, and conditions outside.
   - Natural: The weather affects my mood more than I expected.
   - IT/pro: Bad weather can affect commuting and office attendance.
   - Mistake: Avoid `the weather is rain`; say `it's rainy` or `it's raining`.

2. **sun / sunshine / sunny**
   - Meaning: light from the sun.
   - Natural: I like sunny weather when it's not too hot.
   - IT/pro: A short walk in the sunshine helps me reset after screen-heavy work.
   - Mistake: Avoid `sun day`; say `sunny day`.

3. **rain / rain / rainy**
   - Meaning: water falling from clouds.
   - Natural: Rainy weather makes traffic worse, but it can feel peaceful at home.
   - IT/pro: Heavy rain can make commuting stressful before morning meetings.
   - Mistake: Avoid `rainy is happen`; say `it's raining`.

4. **heat / heat / hot**
   - Meaning: high temperature.
   - Natural: I don't like extreme heat because it drains my energy.
   - IT/pro: Hot weather makes it harder to focus if the room isn't comfortable.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I feel hot weather`; say `I feel hot` or `the weather is hot`.

5. **humidity / humidity / humid**
   - Meaning: wetness in the air.
   - Natural: Humid weather can feel more uncomfortable than dry heat.
   - IT/pro: In humid weather, I prefer working indoors with good ventilation.
   - Mistake: Avoid `the weather is humidity`; say `the weather is humid`.

6. **relax / relaxation / relaxing**
   - Meaning: rest and feel calm.
   - Natural: Spending time in nature is relaxing for me.
   - IT/pro: Outdoor activities help me relax after long coding sessions.
   - Mistake: Avoid `make me relaxing`; say `helps me relax`.

7. **exercise / exercise / active**
   - Meaning: move your body for health.
   - Natural: Outdoor exercise feels better when the weather is cool.
   - IT/pro: Walking outside is a simple way to stay active during a busy work week.
   - Mistake: Avoid `do sport outside` for general meaning; say `exercise outside`.

8. **environment / environment / environmental**
   - Meaning: natural world or surrounding conditions.
   - Natural: Green spaces make the city environment more pleasant.
   - IT/pro: A good work environment includes light, air, and enough breaks from screens.
   - Mistake: Avoid `environmental is good`; say `the environment is good`.

### 2e. Common Vietnamese Learner Mistakes

```text
Mistake: The weather is rain.
Better: It's rainy. / It's raining.
Why: Use adjective "rainy" or verb "raining".
```

```text
Mistake: I like cool air weather.
Better: I like cool weather.
Why: "Cool weather" is the natural phrase.
```

```text
Mistake: I go outside for breathe fresh air.
Better: I go outside to breathe fresh air.
Why: Use "to + verb" for purpose.
```

```text
Mistake: Nature makes me relaxing.
Better: Nature helps me relax. / Nature is relaxing.
Why: Use "helps me + verb" or adjective "relaxing".
```

```text
Mistake: I often go to outside.
Better: I often go outside.
Why: No "to" before outside.
```

```text
Mistake: There are many green in my city.
Better: There are many green spaces in my city.
Why: Use noun phrase "green spaces".
```

```text
Mistake: The weather affects to my mood.
Better: The weather affects my mood.
Why: "Affect" does not need "to".
```

```text
Mistake: I very like sunny day.
Better: I really like sunny days.
Why: Use "really" before verbs and plural for general habit.
```

## Part 4 - Speaking Patterns, Grammar & Expression Tips

### Pattern 1: Weather Preference With Reason

```text
I prefer cool weather because it makes me feel more active.
```

Use it for:

```text
I prefer + weather + because...
```

### Pattern 2: Weather And Routine

```text
Heavy rain can affect my routine because commuting becomes harder.
```

Use it for:

```text
Weather condition + can affect my routine because...
```

### Pattern 3: Screen Balance

```text
Because I work with screens all day, outdoor breaks help me clear my head.
```

Use it for:

```text
Because I work with screens all day, ...
```

### Pattern 4: Balanced Rain Answer

```text
Rain is inconvenient when I need to travel, but it can feel peaceful at home.
```

Use it for:

```text
X is inconvenient when..., but...
```

### Grammar Focus 1: Weather Adjectives

Use adjective forms naturally.

```text
It's sunny today.
It's rainy in the afternoon.
The weather is hot and humid.
The air feels cool and fresh.
```

Upgrade:

```text
Basic: The weather is rain.
Better: It's rainy today, so I may stay indoors.
```

### Grammar Focus 2: Cause And Effect

Use it to connect weather with lifestyle.

```text
Hot weather makes me feel tired.
Fresh air helps me relax.
Heavy rain can change my plans.
```

Speaking drill:

```text
Use `makes me` and `helps me` to describe your favorite weather.
```

### Grammar Focus 3: Comparison

Use it to compare seasons or places.

```text
Cool weather is more comfortable than extreme heat.
Parks are more relaxing than crowded shopping malls.
Compared to my hometown, this city has fewer green spaces.
```

Speaking drill:

```text
Compare weather in two cities or seasons you know.
```

### Answer Patterns

Part 1:

```text
Direct answer -> weather/place -> reason -> small routine example
```

Part 2:

```text
What place/activity -> where/when -> what you do -> how it makes you feel -> why it matters
```

Part 3:

```text
General opinion -> health/lifestyle reason -> city example -> balanced conclusion
```

### Expression Tips

- Use `It's rainy` or `It's raining`, not `the weather is rain`.
- Use `hot and humid` for common Vietnamese weather.
- Use `clear my head`, `improve my mood`, and `disconnect from screens` for lifestyle answers.
- Use `green spaces` for parks, trees, and public natural areas.
- Avoid only saying weather is beautiful; explain what it lets you do.

### Repair Strategies

```text
I don't know the exact temperature, but it feels hot and humid.
What I mean is, the weather affects my energy.
A simple example would be walking in the evening.
Let me put it another way: nature helps me slow down.
```

### Model Answer

```text
I'd like to talk about a small park near where I live. It's not a famous place, but I enjoy visiting it because it's convenient and peaceful.

I usually go there in the evening, especially after a long day of working on my laptop. I don't do anything complicated. I just walk slowly, listen to music sometimes, and enjoy the fresh air. If the weather is cool and fresh, the walk feels even better.

The main reason I enjoy this place is that it helps me disconnect from screens. As a developer, I spend a lot of time reading code, joining meetings, and checking messages. After several hours indoors, even a short outdoor break can clear my head.

Another reason is that the park makes the city feel less crowded. There are trees, benches, and enough space for people to walk or exercise. It reminds me that a healthier lifestyle doesn't always require a big plan. Sometimes a simple walk in a green space is enough.

Overall, I like this park because it's practical, relaxing, and easy to include in my routine.
```
