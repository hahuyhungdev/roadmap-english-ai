## Part 2 - Vocabulary In Context

### 2a. Word Families

1. **remember / memory / memorable**
   - Meaning: keep something from the past in your mind.
   - Natural: One of my most memorable childhood experiences was playing outside with my cousins.
   - IT/pro: I still remember the first time I used a computer because it made me curious about technology.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I have a memory when`; say `I remember when` or `one memory I have is`.

2. **grow / growth / grown-up**
   - Meaning: become older, more mature, or more developed.
   - Natural: I grew up in a fairly quiet neighborhood.
   - IT/pro: Looking back, small childhood interests can shape your grown-up career choices.
   - Mistake: Avoid `when I grow`; say `when I was growing up` for the past.

3. **experience / experienced / inexperienced**
   - Meaning: something that happened to you and affected you.
   - Natural: That experience taught me to be more patient.
   - IT/pro: Early experiences with problem-solving helped me become more confident as a developer.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I experienced a memory`; say `I had an experience` or `I remember`.

4. **friend / friendship / friendly**
   - Meaning: people you like and spend time with.
   - Natural: Childhood friendships were simpler because we didn't overthink things.
   - IT/pro: Childhood teamwork, like group projects, can teach basic communication.
   - Mistake: Avoid `make friend with`; say `make friends with`.

5. **teach / teacher / lesson**
   - Meaning: help someone learn something.
   - Natural: A strict teacher taught me to take responsibility for my own work.
   - IT/pro: A good teacher can make a difficult subject feel less scary, just like good documentation does.
   - Mistake: Avoid `teach me a knowledge`; say `teach me something` or `give me knowledge`.

6. **change / changeable / unchanged**
   - Meaning: become different over time.
   - Natural: I've changed a lot since I was a child, especially in how I handle pressure.
   - IT/pro: My interests changed from playing with computers to building real software.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I changed myself a lot`; say `I've changed a lot`.

7. **support / supportive / supportively**
   - Meaning: help someone emotionally or practically.
   - Natural: My family was supportive, even when they didn't fully understand my interests.
   - IT/pro: Supportive people make it easier to explore a new skill or career path.
   - Mistake: Avoid `my family supported for me`; say `my family supported me`.

8. **nostalgia / nostalgic / nostalgically**
   - Meaning: a warm feeling when thinking about the past.
   - Natural: I feel nostalgic when I hear old songs from my school days.
   - IT/pro: Old games and early computers make me nostalgic because they remind me why I got interested in tech.
   - Mistake: Avoid overusing `nostalgic` for every memory; use it when the feeling is warm and personal.

### 2e. Common Vietnamese Learner Mistakes

```text
Mistake: When I was child, I very happy.
Better: When I was a child, I was very happy.
Why: Use "a child" and add the verb "was".
```

```text
Mistake: I have many memories when I study primary school.
Better: I have many memories from primary school.
Why: Use "from + period/place" for memories.
```

```text
Mistake: I used to played outside.
Better: I used to play outside.
Why: After "used to", use the base verb.
```

```text
Mistake: I was very like computer.
Better: I really liked computers.
Why: Use "really" before verbs, and use past tense.
```

```text
Mistake: My parents gave me many pressure.
Better: My parents put a lot of pressure on me.
Why: Use "put pressure on someone".
```

```text
Mistake: It made me became independent.
Better: It made me become independent.
Why: After "make + object", use the base verb.
```

```text
Mistake: I miss about my childhood.
Better: I miss my childhood.
Why: "Miss" takes a direct object.
```

```text
Mistake: I didn't knew it was important.
Better: I didn't know it was important.
Why: After "didn't", use the base verb.
```

## Part 4 - Speaking Patterns, Grammar & Expression Tips

### Pattern 1: Past Habit With Used To

```text
I used to play outside a lot when I was younger.
```

Use it for:

```text
I used to + base verb...
```

Common mistake:

```text
Avoid: I used to played outside.
Better: I used to play outside.
```

### Pattern 2: Past Story With Time Marker

```text
When I was in primary school, I had a teacher who made me more confident.
```

Use it for:

```text
When I was... / Back then... / At that age...
```

### Pattern 3: Looking Back

```text
Looking back, I think that experience taught me to be more patient.
```

Use it for:

```text
Looking back, I think...
```

### Pattern 4: Change Over Time

```text
I've changed a lot since childhood, especially in the way I handle pressure.
```

Use it for:

```text
I've changed a lot since..., especially in...
```

### Grammar Focus: Past Simple, Used To, And Present Perfect

Use **past simple** for one completed event:

```text
I got my first computer when I was in secondary school.
```

Use **used to** for past habits:

```text
I used to spend hours trying different programs.
```

Use **present perfect** for change from past to now:

```text
I've become more confident since then.
```

### Expression Tips

- Don't make every childhood answer extremely emotional. Ordinary memories are acceptable if you explain why they matter.
- Use time markers: when I was growing up, back then, at that age, looking back.
- Add a lesson or value at the end of a story.
- If the memory is private, keep the answer general and focus on what you learned.
- For IT context, connect early curiosity, computers, problem-solving, independence, or confidence.

### Part 2 Cue Card Practice

#### Cue Card

```text

- what the memory is
- when and where it happened
- who was with you
```

#### Planning Notes

```text
Memory: playing outside after school / using a computer for the first time / a helpful teacher
Time: primary school / secondary school / when I was around...
People: family / cousins / classmates / teacher
Why: simple happiness / curiosity / confidence / lesson learned
```

#### Model Answer

```text
One childhood memory I remember clearly is the first time I used a computer. I think I was in secondary school, and it happened at a relative's house. The computer itself wasn't special by today's standards, but at that time it felt completely new to me.

I remember clicking around, opening different programs, and trying to understand what each thing did. I didn't know any technical words, and I definitely didn't think I would become a software engineer later. I was just curious and excited.

The reason this memory stayed with me is that it shows where one part of my personality came from. I've always liked figuring things out, even when I don't understand them at first. Looking back, that small moment helped shape my interest in technology. It didn't decide my whole future, of course, but it was one of the early experiences that made me curious about computers and problem-solving.
```

#### Upgrade Notes

- Start with a clear memory.
- Add time, place, and people.
- Explain why it mattered.
- End with a lesson, value, or connection to who you are now.
