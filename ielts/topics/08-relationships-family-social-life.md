## Part 2 - Vocabulary In Context

### 2a. Word Families

1. **relationship / relate / related**
   - Meaning: connection between people.
   - Natural: Strong relationships need time and effort.
   - IT/pro: Good working relationships make collaboration smoother.
   - Mistake: Avoid `relationship with my friend is good`; say `I have a good relationship with my friend`.

2. **friendship / friend / friendly**
   - Meaning: relationship between friends.
   - Natural: Friendship in adulthood often requires more intentional effort.
   - IT/pro: A friendly team culture makes it easier to ask questions.
   - Mistake: Avoid `make relationship with friends`; say `build friendships`.

3. **connection / connect / connected**
   - Meaning: feeling close or linked.
   - Natural: Real connection is harder when people are always distracted.
   - IT/pro: Remote teams need intentional communication to stay connected.
   - Mistake: Avoid `connect with each other very close`; say `feel close to each other`.

4. **boundary / bound / set boundaries**
   - Meaning: a limit that protects time, energy, or comfort.
   - Natural: Healthy boundaries help people stay kind without feeling exhausted.
   - IT/pro: I set boundaries around work messages after office hours.
   - Mistake: Avoid `make boundary`; say `set boundaries`.

5. **expectation / expect / expected**
   - Meaning: what people believe should happen.
   - Natural: Family expectations can be supportive, but sometimes stressful.
   - IT/pro: Clear expectations prevent conflict in a team.
   - Mistake: Avoid `my family expect me success`; say `my family expects me to be successful`.

6. **generation / generational / generation gap**
   - Meaning: people born in different periods.
   - Natural: A generation gap can affect how families talk about career choices.
   - IT/pro: Different generations may have different views on remote work or AI.
   - Mistake: Avoid `different generation have`; say `different generations have`.

7. **support / supportive / support system**
   - Meaning: help, care, or encouragement.
   - Natural: A good support system helps people handle difficult periods.
   - IT/pro: Supportive colleagues make stressful projects easier.
   - Mistake: Avoid `my family support for me`; say `my family supports me`.

8. **conflict / conflict / conflicting**
   - Meaning: disagreement or tension.
   - Natural: Conflict is normal, but people need to handle it respectfully.
   - IT/pro: Conflicting priorities can create tension between teams.
   - Mistake: Avoid `conflict with opinion`; say `a conflict of opinions`.

9. **priority / prioritize / priorities**
   - Meaning: what matters most.
   - Natural: Friendships change when people's priorities change.
   - IT/pro: I prioritize urgent work, but I also try to protect personal time.
   - Mistake: Avoid `my priority is changed`; say `my priorities have changed`.

10. **understanding / understand / mutual understanding**
    - Meaning: ability to see another person's view.
    - Natural: Mutual understanding is more important than always agreeing.
    - IT/pro: Good documentation creates shared understanding in a team.
    - Mistake: Avoid `understand each other opinion`; say `understand each other's opinions`.

### 2e. Common Vietnamese Learner Mistakes

```text
Mistake: I and my friend are very close each other.
Better: My friend and I are very close. / We're very close to each other.
Why: Put "I" after the other person and use a natural structure.
```

```text
Mistake: I have many relationships.
Better: I have many friends. / I have a wide social circle.
Why: "Relationships" can sound romantic or formal.
```

```text
Mistake: My family always care me.
Better: My family always cares about me.
Why: Use "care about".
```

```text
Mistake: I discuss with my parents about career.
Better: I discuss my career with my parents.
Why: "Discuss" does not need "about".
```

```text
Mistake: We contact less than before.
Better: We're in contact less often than before.
Why: Use "be in contact" or "keep in touch".
```

```text
Mistake: I afraid to say no.
Better: I'm afraid to say no.
Why: Add "am" before adjective.
```

```text
Mistake: They have different thinking with me.
Better: They think differently from me. / They have a different view from mine.
Why: Use natural comparison.
```

```text
Mistake: I need private space.
Better: I need personal space.
Why: "Personal space" is the natural phrase.
```

## Part 4 - Speaking Patterns, Grammar & Expression Tips

### 4a. Sentence Patterns

1. **As I have got older, I have become more...**
2. **I'm close to..., but we don't...**
3. **One thing I value in a relationship is...**
4. **I try to be honest without being...**
5. **The main difference between generations is...**

### 4b. Grammar Points To Review

#### Grammar 1: Present Perfect For Change Over Time

```text
My priorities have changed as I have got older.
I have become more careful about my personal time.
```

#### Grammar 2: Comparatives For Past And Present

```text
I'm less social than I was in university.
Friendships are harder to maintain than before.
```

#### Grammar 3: Relative Clauses

```text
I value friends who are honest and supportive.
I respect people who can listen without judging.
```

#### Grammar 4: Concession With "Although"

```text
Although my parents and I have different views, I know they care about me.
Although small talk isn't deep, it can make people comfortable.
```

#### Grammar 5: Gerunds As Subjects

```text
Setting boundaries is important.
Listening carefully can prevent conflict.
```

### 4c. Answer Patterns

```text
Change over time -> reason -> example -> feeling
As I have got older, my friendships have become more intentional because everyone is busy. For example, I now make plans in advance instead of waiting naturally. I actually value that more.
```

```text
Balanced view -> respect -> personal boundary
I respect my family's opinions, but I also need to make choices that fit my own life.
```

### 4d. Expression Tips

- Say **My friend and I**, not **I and my friend**.
- Use **stay in touch** for keeping contact.
- Use **drift apart** when people become less close naturally.
- Say **set boundaries**, not **make boundaries**.
- Avoid making family answers too negative; balance honesty with respect.

### 4e. Upgrade Pattern

```text
Basic: My friends are busy, so we meet less.
Better: As we have got older, my friends and I have become busier, so we need to make time for each other more intentionally.
Strong: As we have got older, my friends and I have different priorities, so maintaining friendship takes mutual effort, honest communication, and realistic expectations.
```

### 4f. Repair Strategies

```text
If you forget "boundary":
"I mean a personal limit..."
Then say: "a boundary."
```

```text
If your answer feels too private:
Make it general: "I think many people experience this..."
```

```text
If your answer is too short:
Add contrast: "It was easier before, but now..."
```

### 5b. IELTS Speaking Part 2 Cue Card

Describe a person you have a good relationship with.

You should say:

- who this person is
- how you know this person
- what makes the relationship strong
- and explain why this relationship is important to you

### Model Answer

```text
I'd like to talk about a close friend from my working life. We first knew each other through software projects, but over time the relationship became more personal and supportive.

What makes the relationship strong is that we can communicate honestly. We don't need to talk every day, but when we do talk, the conversation is usually meaningful. We can discuss work, personal goals, and sometimes difficult decisions without judging each other too quickly.

Another thing I value is mutual effort. As adults, both of us are busy, so staying close doesn't happen automatically. We try to stay in touch, share useful ideas, and support each other when work becomes stressful.

This relationship is important to me because it gives me a sense of understanding. In modern life, people can have many contacts but still feel not fully understood. A relationship with trust, honesty, and emotional support is rare, so I really appreciate it.
```
