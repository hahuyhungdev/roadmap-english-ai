## Part 2 - Vocabulary In Context

### 2a. Word Families

1. **relationship / relate / related**
   - Meaning: connection between people.
   - Natural: Strong relationships need time and effort.
   - IT/pro: Good working relationships make collaboration smoother.
   - Mistake: Avoid `relationship with my friend is good`; say `I have a good relationship with my friend`.

2. **friendship / friend / friendly**
   - Meaning: relationship between friends.
   - Natural: Friendship in adulthood often requires more intentional effort.
   - IT/pro: A friendly team culture makes it easier to ask questions.
   - Mistake: Avoid `make relationship with friends`; say `build friendships`.

3. **connection / connect / connected**
   - Meaning: feeling close or linked.
   - Natural: Real connection is harder when people are always distracted.
   - IT/pro: Remote teams need intentional communication to stay connected.
   - Mistake: Avoid `connect with each other very close`; say `feel close to each other`.

4. **boundary / bound / set boundaries**
   - Meaning: a limit that protects time, energy, or comfort.
   - Natural: Healthy boundaries help people stay kind without feeling exhausted.
   - IT/pro: I set boundaries around work messages after office hours.
   - Mistake: Avoid `make boundary`; say `set boundaries`.

5. **expectation / expect / expected**
   - Meaning: what people believe should happen.
   - Natural: Family expectations can be supportive, but sometimes stressful.
   - IT/pro: Clear expectations prevent conflict in a team.
   - Mistake: Avoid `my family expect me success`; say `my family expects me to be successful`.

6. **generation / generational / generation gap**
   - Meaning: people born in different periods.
   - Natural: A generation gap can affect how families talk about career choices.
   - IT/pro: Different generations may have different views on remote work or AI.
   - Mistake: Avoid `different generation have`; say `different generations have`.

7. **support / supportive / support system**
   - Meaning: help, care, or encouragement.
   - Natural: A good support system helps people handle difficult periods.
   - IT/pro: Supportive colleagues make stressful projects easier.
   - Mistake: Avoid `my family support for me`; say `my family supports me`.

8. **conflict / conflict / conflicting**
   - Meaning: disagreement or tension.
   - Natural: Conflict is normal, but people need to handle it respectfully.
   - IT/pro: Conflicting priorities can create tension between teams.
   - Mistake: Avoid `conflict with opinion`; say `a conflict of opinions`.

9. **priority / prioritize / priorities**
   - Meaning: what matters most.
   - Natural: Friendships change when people's priorities change.
   - IT/pro: I prioritize urgent work, but I also try to protect personal time.
   - Mistake: Avoid `my priority is changed`; say `my priorities have changed`.

10. **understanding / understand / mutual understanding**
    - Meaning: ability to see another person's view.
    - Natural: Mutual understanding is more important than always agreeing.
    - IT/pro: Good documentation creates shared understanding in a team.
    - Mistake: Avoid `understand each other opinion`; say `understand each other's opinions`.

### 2e. Common Vietnamese Learner Mistakes

```text
Mistake: I and my friend are very close each other.
Better: My friend and I are very close. / We are very close to each other.
Why: Put "I" after the other person and use a natural structure.
```

```text
Mistake: I have many relationships.
Better: I have many friends. / I have a wide social circle.
Why: "Relationships" can sound romantic or formal.
```

```text
Mistake: My family always care me.
Better: My family always cares about me.
Why: Use "care about".
```

```text
Mistake: I discuss with my parents about career.
Better: I discuss my career with my parents.
Why: "Discuss" does not need "about".
```

```text
Mistake: We contact less than before.
Better: We are in contact less often than before.
Why: Use "be in contact" or "keep in touch".
```

```text
Mistake: I afraid to say no.
Better: I am afraid to say no.
Why: Add "am" before adjective.
```

```text
Mistake: They have different thinking with me.
Better: They think differently from me. / They have a different view from mine.
Why: Use natural comparison.
```

```text
Mistake: I need private space.
Better: I need personal space.
Why: "Personal space" is the natural phrase.
```

## Part 3 - Core Idea Bank

### Idea 1: Adult Friendship Needs Intention

Simple version:

```text
Friendship becomes harder when people get older.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I think friendship in adulthood needs more intentional effort. People are busy with work, family, and personal goals, so staying close does not happen automatically.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
After work, I may be tired, so I need to make time for friends instead of waiting until I feel completely free.
```

Useful language:

```text
intentional effort / stay close / make time for someone
```

### Idea 2: Small Talk Has A Purpose

Simple version:

```text
Small talk can be useful.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Small talk may seem shallow, but it can create comfort before deeper conversations. It helps people start a connection without making the situation too serious too quickly.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
In meetings, a little small talk can make collaboration feel more human, especially in remote teams.
```

Useful language:

```text
create comfort / start a connection / remote teams
```

### Idea 3: Deep Conversations Are Rare Because Attention Is Rare

Simple version:

```text
People do not always listen deeply.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Real conversations are rare partly because many people are distracted. To feel genuinely understood, we need attention, patience, and emotional availability.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
Even at work, better listening can prevent misunderstandings and unnecessary conflict.
```

Useful language:

```text
genuinely understood / emotional availability / better listening
```

### Idea 4: Boundaries Protect Relationships

Simple version:

```text
Boundaries are not selfish.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I do not see boundaries as selfish. In fact, healthy boundaries can protect relationships because people become less resentful when they are honest about their limits.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
If I cannot reply to work messages late at night, it is better to set that expectation clearly.
```

Useful language:

```text
healthy boundaries / honest about limits / less resentful
```

### Idea 5: Family Expectations Can Be Both Supportive And Stressful

Simple version:

```text
Family expectations can help or pressure people.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Family expectations often come from care, but they can still create pressure. The challenge is to respect family while also choosing a path that fits your own life.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
Some families prefer stable traditional careers, while tech careers can look uncertain from the outside.
```

Useful language:

```text
come from care / create pressure / choose my own path
```

### Idea 6: The Generation Gap Is Often About Experience

Simple version:

```text
Different generations see life differently.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I think the generation gap is not only about age. It is also about different experiences, economic conditions, and ideas of success.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
Older generations may value job stability more, while younger workers may care more about growth, flexibility, and meaningful work.
```

Useful language:

```text
generation gap / job stability / different experiences
```

### Idea 7: Conflict Can Be Healthy If Handled Well

Simple version:

```text
Conflict is not always bad.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Conflict can be healthy if people handle it respectfully. The problem is not disagreement itself, but blaming, avoiding, or refusing to listen.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
In product discussions, disagreement can improve decisions if everyone focuses on the problem instead of ego.
```

Useful language:

```text
handle conflict calmly / focus on the problem / respect different opinions
```

### Idea 8: Quality Matters More Than Quantity

Simple version:

```text
It is better to have a few close relationships.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
For me, the quality of relationships matters more than the number of people I know. A few supportive and honest relationships can be more valuable than a large but shallow social circle.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
At work, I also prefer trust and clear communication over just having many connections.
```

Useful language:

```text
quality over quantity / supportive relationships / shallow social circle
```

## Part 4 - Speaking Patterns, Grammar & Expression Tips

### 4a. Sentence Patterns

1. **As I have got older, I have become more...**
2. **I am close to..., but we do not...**
3. **One thing I value in a relationship is...**
4. **I try to be honest without being...**
5. **The main difference between generations is...**

### 4b. Grammar Points To Review

#### Grammar 1: Present Perfect For Change Over Time

```text
My priorities have changed as I have got older.
I have become more careful about my personal time.
```

#### Grammar 2: Comparatives For Past And Present

```text
I am less social than I was in university.
Friendships are harder to maintain than before.
```

#### Grammar 3: Relative Clauses

```text
I value friends who are honest and supportive.
I respect people who can listen without judging.
```

#### Grammar 4: Concession With "Although"

```text
Although my parents and I have different views, I know they care about me.
Although small talk is not deep, it can make people comfortable.
```

#### Grammar 5: Gerunds As Subjects

```text
Setting boundaries is important.
Listening carefully can prevent conflict.
```

### 4c. Answer Patterns

```text
Change over time -> reason -> example -> feeling
As I have got older, my friendships have become more intentional because everyone is busy. For example, I now make plans in advance instead of waiting naturally. I actually value that more.
```

```text
Balanced view -> respect -> personal boundary
I respect my family's opinions, but I also need to make choices that fit my own life.
```

### 4d. Expression Tips

- Say **My friend and I**, not **I and my friend**.
- Use **stay in touch** for keeping contact.
- Use **drift apart** when people become less close naturally.
- Say **set boundaries**, not **make boundaries**.
- Avoid making family answers too negative; balance honesty with respect.

### 4e. Upgrade Pattern

```text
Basic: My friends are busy, so we meet less.
Better: As we have got older, my friends and I have become busier, so we need to make time for each other more intentionally.
Strong: As we have got older, my friends and I have different priorities, so maintaining friendship takes mutual effort, honest communication, and realistic expectations.
```

### 4f. Repair Strategies

```text
If you forget "boundary":
"I mean a personal limit..."
Then say: "a boundary."
```

```text
If your answer feels too private:
Make it general: "I think many people experience this..."
```

```text
If your answer is too short:
Add contrast: "It was easier before, but now..."
```

## Part 5 - Guided Speaking Practice

### 5a. IELTS Speaking Part 1 Practice

#### Question 1: Do you have many close friends?

```text
Not many, but I have a few close friends. For me, quality matters more than having a large social circle.
```

#### Question 2: How do you keep in touch with friends?

```text
Mostly through messages, but I try to meet them in person when possible. Real conversations feel different from online contact.
```

#### Question 3: Do you enjoy small talk?

```text
I used to dislike it, but now I think it has a purpose. It helps people feel comfortable before a deeper conversation.
```

#### Question 4: Are you close to your family?

```text
Yes, I am close to them, although we do not always share the same opinions. I try to respect their views and explain mine calmly.
```

#### Question 5: Do you think friendships change over time?

```text
Yes, definitely. As people get older, their priorities change, so friendships need more intentional effort.
```

#### Question 6: Is it easy for you to say no?

```text
Not always. I sometimes feel guilty, but I am learning to set boundaries more clearly and politely.
```

### 5b. IELTS Speaking Part 2 Cue Card

Describe a person you have a good relationship with.

You should say:

- who this person is
- how you know this person
- what makes the relationship strong
- and explain why this relationship is important to you

### Model Answer

```text
I would like to talk about a close friend from my working life. We first knew each other through software projects, but over time the relationship became more personal and supportive.

What makes the relationship strong is that we can communicate honestly. We do not need to talk every day, but when we do talk, the conversation is usually meaningful. We can discuss work, personal goals, and sometimes difficult decisions without judging each other too quickly.

Another thing I value is mutual effort. As adults, both of us are busy, so staying close does not happen automatically. We try to stay in touch, share useful ideas, and support each other when work becomes stressful.

This relationship is important to me because it gives me a sense of understanding. In modern life, people can have many contacts but still feel not fully understood. A relationship with trust, honesty, and emotional support is rare, so I really appreciate it.
```

### Shadowing Version

```text
The relationship became more personal and supportive.
We can communicate honestly.
Staying close does not happen automatically.
I value mutual effort.
A relationship with trust and honesty is rare.
```

### 5c. IELTS Speaking Part 3 Practice

#### Question 1: Why is it harder to maintain friendships as adults?

```text
It is harder because adults have more responsibilities, like work, family, money, and personal goals. People may still care about each other, but they need more intentional effort to stay in touch.
```

#### Question 2: Are online relationships as strong as face-to-face relationships?

```text
They can be strong, but I think face-to-face relationships usually create deeper connection. Online communication is convenient, but it is easier to misunderstand tone or become distracted.
```

#### Question 3: Why do family members from different generations often disagree?

```text
They often disagree because they grew up in different conditions. For example, older generations may value stability, while younger people may value flexibility or personal growth.
```

#### Question 4: Is it important to set boundaries in relationships?

```text
Yes, because boundaries protect both people. If someone always says yes and hides their limits, they may become resentful later. Honest boundaries can make relationships healthier.
```

#### Question 5: What makes communication effective?

```text
Effective communication needs honesty, listening, and respect. It is not only about expressing your opinion clearly, but also about understanding the other person's point of view.
```
