## Part 2 - Vocabulary In Context

### 2a. Word Families

1. **stress / stressful / stressed**
   - Meaning: pressure that affects body or mind.
   - Natural: Work can be stressful when deadlines are tight.
   - IT/pro: Debugging a production issue can be stressful because the impact is real.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I am stress`; say `I am stressed`.

2. **health / healthy / unhealthy**
   - Meaning: physical and mental condition.
   - Natural: A healthy routine does not need to be extreme.
   - IT/pro: Sitting all day is unhealthy if I never move or take breaks.
   - Mistake: Avoid `my healthy is good`; say `my health is good`.

3. **recovery / recover / recovered**
   - Meaning: returning to a better condition after stress or tiredness.
   - Natural: Recovery is part of productivity, not a waste of time.
   - IT/pro: After an intense release, the team needs time to recover.
   - Mistake: Avoid `recover my energy`; say `recover from stress` or `restore my energy`.

4. **burnout / burn out / burned out**
   - Meaning: long-term exhaustion from too much pressure.
   - Natural: Burnout often happens when people ignore small warning signs.
   - IT/pro: Constant urgent work can make developers burn out.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I am burnout`; say `I am burned out`.

5. **discipline / disciplined / self-discipline**
   - Meaning: ability to keep healthy habits.
   - Natural: Discipline should support health, not punish the body.
   - IT/pro: Self-discipline helps me take breaks before I get completely exhausted.
   - Mistake: Avoid `do discipline`; say `build discipline`.

6. **balance / balanced / imbalance**
   - Meaning: a healthy mix of work, rest, and personal life.
   - Natural: A balanced lifestyle is easier to maintain than a strict one.
   - IT/pro: Work-life balance helps me keep my energy stable.
   - Mistake: Avoid `balance life`; say `balance work and life`.

7. **sleep / sleepy / sleep quality**
   - Meaning: rest at night.
   - Natural: Sleep quality affects my mood and focus.
   - IT/pro: If I sleep badly, I make more mistakes when coding.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I sleep late night`; say `I go to bed late`.

8. **exercise / exercise / physical activity**
   - Meaning: movement for health.
   - Natural: Light exercise is better than no movement at all.
   - IT/pro: A short walk after coding helps me reset my mind.
   - Mistake: Avoid `play exercise`; say `do exercise` or `work out`.

9. **sustainable / sustain / sustainability**
   - Meaning: something you can keep doing for a long time.
   - Natural: I prefer a sustainable routine to an extreme fitness plan.
   - IT/pro: A sustainable pace prevents burnout in long projects.
   - Mistake: Avoid `sustain lifestyle`; say `maintain a sustainable lifestyle`.

10. **well-being / well / wellness**
    - Meaning: general physical and mental health.
    - Natural: Well-being includes rest, relationships, and mental health.
    - IT/pro: Companies should care about employee well-being, not just output.
    - Mistake: Avoid `wellbeing is only gym`; say `well-being is broader than fitness`.

### 2e. Common Vietnamese Learner Mistakes

```text
Mistake: I am stress because my job.
Better: I am stressed because of my job. / My job is stressful.
Why: Use "stressed" for feeling and "stressful" for the thing.
```

```text
Mistake: I go to sleep very late night.
Better: I go to bed very late at night.
Why: "Go to bed" is the natural action.
```

```text
Mistake: I do gym every day.
Better: I go to the gym every day. / I work out every day.
Why: Use the natural phrase.
```

```text
Mistake: My body is very tiredness.
Better: My body feels very tired. / I feel exhausted.
Why: "Tiredness" is a noun; use adjective after "feel".
```

```text
Mistake: I need relax after work.
Better: I need to relax after work.
Why: Use "need to + verb".
```

```text
Mistake: I have burnout.
Better: I am burned out. / I am experiencing burnout.
Why: Use adjective or noun phrase correctly.
```

```text
Mistake: Healthy is important.
Better: Health is important. / Being healthy is important.
Why: "Healthy" is an adjective.
```

```text
Mistake: I reduce using phone.
Better: I reduce my phone use. / I reduce the time I spend on my phone.
Why: Use a noun phrase after "reduce".
```

## Part 3 - Core Idea Bank

### Idea 1: Health Is Not Only Exercise

Simple version:

```text
Health includes many things, not only sport.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I think health is not only about exercise. It also includes sleep, stress, screen time, relationships, and the ability to recover after pressure.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
As a developer, I may not do heavy physical work, but sitting for long hours and thinking intensely can still affect my health.
```

Useful language:

```text
sleep quality / mental energy / recover after pressure
```

### Idea 2: Stress Can Be Useful In Small Amounts

Simple version:

```text
Some stress can help people focus.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
A small amount of stress can be useful because it pushes people to take action. However, if stress becomes constant, it damages both health and work quality.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
A release deadline can help the team focus, but constant urgent work usually leads to mistakes.
```

Useful language:

```text
constant stress / work quality / take action
```

### Idea 3: Recovery Is Productive

Simple version:

```text
Rest helps people work better later.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Many people treat rest as laziness, but I see recovery as part of productivity. If I never recharge, I cannot think clearly or communicate well.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
After a long debugging session, a short walk can help me see the problem more clearly.
```

Useful language:

```text
recharge mentally / think clearly / part of productivity
```

### Idea 4: Sustainable Habits Beat Extreme Plans

Simple version:

```text
Small healthy habits are easier to keep.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I prefer sustainable habits because extreme plans usually fail when life gets busy. A short walk, better sleep, and less screen time at night are simple but realistic.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
I do not need a perfect fitness routine; I need habits that survive busy workdays.
```

Useful language:

```text
sustainable habits / realistic routine / survive busy days
```

### Idea 5: Screen Time Needs Boundaries

Simple version:

```text
Too much screen time is not good.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Screen time is difficult to avoid because work, study, and entertainment all happen online. So instead of trying to remove it completely, I try to set boundaries around it.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
I use screens for coding, so I try not to spend the whole evening scrolling after work.
```

Useful language:

```text
set boundaries / reduce screen time / scrolling after work
```

### Idea 6: Mental Health Should Be Normal To Discuss

Simple version:

```text
People should talk about stress more openly.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I think mental health should be discussed more normally, especially in high-pressure work. If people hide stress for too long, small problems can become serious.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
In tech teams, burnout can affect communication, decision making, and code quality.
```

Useful language:

```text
talk openly / high-pressure work / warning signs
```

### Idea 7: Self-Care Should Not Become Another Pressure

Simple version:

```text
Self-care should not be stressful.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Sometimes modern health culture makes people feel guilty for not having a perfect routine. I think self-care should be practical and kind, not another source of pressure.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
If I miss exercise during a busy sprint, I try to restart gently instead of blaming myself.
```

Useful language:

```text
feel guilty / practical and kind / restart gently
```

### Idea 8: Companies Influence Employee Health

Simple version:

```text
Workplaces affect people's health.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Individual habits matter, but companies also shape people's health through workload, deadlines, meetings, and culture. A healthy workplace should not depend only on personal discipline.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
If every task is urgent, developers cannot maintain a sustainable pace.
```

Useful language:

```text
healthy workplace / sustainable pace / personal discipline
```

## Part 4 - Speaking Patterns, Grammar & Expression Tips

### 4a. Sentence Patterns

1. **When I feel stressed, I usually...**
2. **One habit that helps me stay healthy is...**
3. **I try to avoid..., especially when...**
4. **If I do not sleep well, I tend to...**
5. **For me, a sustainable lifestyle means...**

### 4b. Grammar Points To Review

#### Grammar 1: Cause And Result

```text
If I sleep badly, I find it harder to concentrate.
Too much screen time can affect my sleep quality.
```

#### Grammar 2: Adjective Choice

```text
I feel stressed.
My work is stressful.
I feel exhausted after a long day.
```

#### Grammar 3: Gerunds After Prepositions

```text
I recover by taking a short walk.
I am careful about working too late.
```

#### Grammar 4: Comparatives

```text
A sustainable routine is better than an extreme plan.
Light exercise is more realistic for me than a strict gym schedule.
```

#### Grammar 5: Modals For Advice

```text
People should protect their sleep.
Companies should avoid creating constant urgency.
```

### 4c. Answer Patterns

```text
Habit -> reason -> example -> result
I take short breaks because they help me recharge. For example, after a long coding session, I walk for a few minutes, and then I can think more clearly.
```

```text
Balanced opinion -> contrast -> personal view
Stress can be useful in small amounts, but constant stress is harmful. Personally, I prefer a sustainable pace.
```

### 4d. Expression Tips

- Say **I feel stressed**, not **I am stress**.
- Use **burned out** for a personal feeling and **burnout** as the noun.
- Say **protect my sleep** when sleep is a priority.
- Use **sustainable pace** for long-term work rhythm.
- Avoid sounding extreme; health answers are stronger when they sound realistic.

### 4e. Upgrade Pattern

```text
Basic: I am stressed, so I sleep.
Better: When I feel stressed, I try to protect my sleep and take short breaks.
Strong: When I feel stressed, I try to protect my sleep, reduce screen time at night, and keep a sustainable routine instead of pushing myself until I burn out.
```

### 4f. Repair Strategies

```text
If you forget "burnout":
"I mean long-term tiredness from too much pressure..."
Then say: "burnout."
```

```text
If your answer sounds too negative:
Add a practical habit: "One thing that helps me is..."
```

```text
If your answer is too general:
Add a work example: "For example, after a long coding session..."
```

## Part 5 - Guided Speaking Practice

### 5a. IELTS Speaking Part 1 Practice

#### Question 1: What do you do to stay healthy?

```text
I try to keep a simple routine. I protect my sleep, take short walks, and avoid spending too much time on screens at night.
```

#### Question 2: Do you often feel stressed?

```text
Sometimes, especially when work is busy or deadlines are tight. But I try to manage stress before it becomes too serious.
```

#### Question 3: Do you think sleep is important?

```text
Yes, definitely. If I do not sleep well, my focus drops and I make more mistakes, especially when I am coding.
```

#### Question 4: How do you relax after work?

```text
I usually take a short walk or do something away from the screen. It helps me clear my head after a long day.
```

#### Question 5: Do you exercise regularly?

```text
Not as much as I should, but I try to move my body every day. For me, light exercise is more sustainable than an extreme plan.
```

#### Question 6: Is modern life stressful?

```text
Yes, I think so. People receive too much information, work long hours, and often feel pressure to be productive all the time.
```

### 5b. IELTS Speaking Part 2 Cue Card

Describe a healthy habit you want to build or maintain.

You should say:

- what the habit is
- why it is important
- how you try to keep it
- and explain how it affects your life

### Model Answer

```text
A healthy habit I want to maintain is protecting my sleep. It sounds simple, but for me it is one of the most important habits because my work depends heavily on focus and mental energy.

In the past, I sometimes stayed up late to code, study, or scroll on my phone. The next day, I could still work, but my thinking was slower and I made more small mistakes. So now I try to reduce screen time at night and avoid doing intense work too late.

I do not have a perfect routine, but I try to keep it realistic. If I have a busy day, I focus on one small thing, like going to bed a bit earlier or taking a short walk after work.

This habit affects my life in a practical way. When I sleep better, I feel less stressed, communicate more patiently, and solve problems more clearly. For me, health is not about being perfect; it is about building a routine that I can maintain in the long run.
```

### Shadowing Version

```text
I want to protect my sleep.
My work depends on focus and mental energy.
I try to reduce screen time at night.
I keep the routine realistic.
Health is about a routine I can maintain.
```

### 5c. IELTS Speaking Part 3 Practice

#### Question 1: Why are many people stressed nowadays?

```text
I think people are stressed because life is full of pressure and information. Many people work long hours, check messages constantly, and feel that they must always improve. This makes it hard to rest properly.
```

#### Question 2: Should schools teach students about health?

```text
Yes, but it should be practical. Students should learn about sleep, stress, exercise, and screen time, not only textbook knowledge about the body.
```

#### Question 3: Do companies have responsibility for employee well-being?

```text
Yes, at least partly. Employees have personal responsibility, but companies control workload, deadlines, and work culture. If the culture creates constant urgency, people may burn out.
```

#### Question 4: Is self-care becoming too commercial?

```text
In some ways, yes. Self-care is sometimes presented as expensive products or perfect routines. But real self-care can be simple, like sleeping enough, setting boundaries, and taking breaks.
```

#### Question 5: How can technology affect health?

```text
Technology can help health by tracking habits or providing useful information. However, too much screen time and constant notifications can affect sleep, attention, and stress levels.
```
