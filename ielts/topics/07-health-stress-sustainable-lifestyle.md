## Part 2 - Vocabulary In Context

### 2a. Word Families

1. **stress / stressful / stressed**
   - Meaning: pressure that affects body or mind.
   - Natural: Work can be stressful when deadlines are tight.
   - IT/pro: Debugging a production issue can be stressful because the impact is real.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I'm stress`; say `I'm stressed`.

2. **health / healthy / unhealthy**
   - Meaning: physical and mental condition.
   - Natural: A healthy routine doesn't need to be extreme.
   - IT/pro: Sitting all day is unhealthy if I never move or take breaks.
   - Mistake: Avoid `my healthy is good`; say `my health is good`.

3. **recovery / recover / recovered**
   - Meaning: returning to a better condition after stress or tiredness.
   - Natural: Recovery is part of productivity, not a waste of time.
   - IT/pro: After an intense release, the team needs time to recover.
   - Mistake: Avoid `recover my energy`; say `recover from stress` or `restore my energy`.

4. **burnout / burn out / burned out**
   - Meaning: long-term exhaustion from too much pressure.
   - Natural: Burnout often happens when people ignore small warning signs.
   - IT/pro: Constant urgent work can make developers burn out.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I'm burnout`; say `I'm burned out`.

5. **discipline / disciplined / self-discipline**
   - Meaning: ability to keep healthy habits.
   - Natural: Discipline should support health, not punish the body.
   - IT/pro: Self-discipline helps me take breaks before I get completely exhausted.
   - Mistake: Avoid `do discipline`; say `build discipline`.

6. **balance / balanced / imbalance**
   - Meaning: a healthy mix of work, rest, and personal life.
   - Natural: A balanced lifestyle is easier to maintain than a strict one.
   - IT/pro: Work-life balance helps me keep my energy stable.
   - Mistake: Avoid `balance life`; say `balance work and life`.

7. **sleep / sleepy / sleep quality**
   - Meaning: rest at night.
   - Natural: Sleep quality affects my mood and focus.
   - IT/pro: If I sleep badly, I make more mistakes when coding.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I sleep late night`; say `I go to bed late`.

8. **exercise / exercise / physical activity**
   - Meaning: movement for health.
   - Natural: Light exercise is better than no movement at all.
   - IT/pro: A short walk after coding helps me reset my mind.
   - Mistake: Avoid `play exercise`; say `do exercise` or `work out`.

9. **sustainable / sustain / sustainability**
   - Meaning: something you can keep doing for a long time.
   - Natural: I prefer a sustainable routine to an extreme fitness plan.
   - IT/pro: A sustainable pace prevents burnout in long projects.
   - Mistake: Avoid `sustain lifestyle`; say `maintain a sustainable lifestyle`.

10. **well-being / well / wellness**
    - Meaning: general physical and mental health.
    - Natural: Well-being includes rest, relationships, and mental health.
    - IT/pro: Companies should care about employee well-being, not just output.
    - Mistake: Avoid `wellbeing is only gym`; say `well-being is broader than fitness`.

### 2e. Common Vietnamese Learner Mistakes

```text
Mistake: I am stress because my job.
Better: I'm stressed because of my job. / My job is stressful.
Why: Use "stressed" for feeling and "stressful" for the thing.
```

```text
Mistake: I go to sleep very late night.
Better: I go to bed very late at night.
Why: "Go to bed" is the natural action.
```

```text
Mistake: I do gym every day.
Better: I go to the gym every day. / I work out every day.
Why: Use the natural phrase.
```

```text
Mistake: My body is very tiredness.
Better: My body feels very tired. / I feel exhausted.
Why: "Tiredness" is a noun; use adjective after "feel".
```

```text
Mistake: I need relax after work.
Better: I need to relax after work.
Why: Use "need to + verb".
```

```text
Mistake: I have burnout.
Better: I'm burned out. / I'm experiencing burnout.
Why: Use adjective or noun phrase correctly.
```

```text
Mistake: Healthy is important.
Better: Health is important. / Being healthy is important.
Why: "Healthy" is an adjective.
```

```text
Mistake: I reduce using phone.
Better: I reduce my phone use. / I reduce the time I spend on my phone.
Why: Use a noun phrase after "reduce".
```

### 4c. Answer Patterns

```text
Habit -> reason -> example -> result
I take short breaks because they help me recharge. For example, after a long coding session, I walk for a few minutes, and then I can think more clearly.
```

```text
Balanced opinion -> contrast -> personal view
Stress can be useful in small amounts, but constant stress is harmful. Personally, I prefer a sustainable pace.
```

### 4d. Expression Tips

- Say **I feel stressed**, not **I'm stress**.
- Use **burned out** for a personal feeling and **burnout** as the noun.
- Say **protect my sleep** when sleep is a priority.
- Use **sustainable pace** for long-term work rhythm.
- Avoid sounding extreme; health answers are stronger when they sound realistic.

### 4e. Upgrade Pattern

```text
Basic: I'm stressed, so I sleep.
Better: When I feel stressed, I try to protect my sleep and take short breaks.
Strong: When I feel stressed, I try to protect my sleep, reduce screen time at night, and keep a sustainable routine instead of pushing myself until I burn out.
```

### 4f. Repair Strategies

```text
If you forget "burnout":
"I mean long-term tiredness from too much pressure..."
Then say: "burnout."
```

```text
If your answer sounds too negative:
Add a practical habit: "One thing that helps me is..."
```

```text
If your answer is too general:
Add a work example: "For example, after a long coding session..."
```

### 5b. IELTS Speaking Part 2 Cue Card

Describe a healthy habit you want to build or maintain.

You should say:

- what the habit is
- why it's important
- how you try to keep it
- and explain how it affects your life

### Model Answer

```text
A healthy habit I wanna maintain is protecting my sleep. It sounds simple, but for me it's one of the most important habits because my work depends heavily on focus and mental energy.

In the past, I sometimes stayed up late to code, study, or scroll on my phone. The next day, I could still work, but my thinking was slower and I made more small mistakes. So now I try to reduce screen time at night and avoid doing intense work too late.

I don't have a perfect routine, but I try to keep it realistic. If I have a busy day, I focus on one small thing, like going to bed a bit earlier or taking a short walk after work.

This habit affects my life in a practical way. When I sleep better, I feel less stressed, communicate more patiently, and solve problems more clearly. For me, health isn't about being perfect; it's about building a routine that I can maintain in the long run.
```

```text
Yes, at least partly. Employees have personal responsibility, but companies control workload, deadlines, and work culture. If the culture creates constant urgency, people may burn out.
```

```text
Technology can help health by tracking habits or providing useful information. However, too much screen time and constant notifications can affect sleep, attention, and stress levels.
```
