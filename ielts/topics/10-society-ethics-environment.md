## Part 2 - Vocabulary In Context

### 2a. Word Families

1. **society / social / socially**
   - Meaning: people living together and shared systems.
   - Natural: Social problems usually need both personal action and public support.
   - IT/pro: Technology products can influence society at a large scale.
   - Mistake: Avoid `in the society`; say `in society` or `in modern society`.

2. **responsibility / responsible / responsibly**
   - Meaning: duty to act carefully.
   - Natural: Everyone has some responsibility for the environment.
   - IT/pro: Engineers should build products responsibly, especially when user data is involved.
   - Mistake: Avoid `take responsibility about`; say `take responsibility for`.

3. **ethics / ethical / ethically**
   - Meaning: ideas about what is right or fair.
   - Natural: Ethical decisions are not always simple.
   - IT/pro: Using personal data ethically is a major issue in digital products.
   - Mistake: Avoid `ethic problem`; say `ethical problem` or `ethical issue`.

4. **privacy / private / privately**
   - Meaning: control over personal information.
   - Natural: Privacy matters because people should control their own data.
   - IT/pro: Apps should collect only the data they really need.
   - Mistake: Avoid `my private is important`; say `my privacy is important`.

5. **environment / environmental / environmentally**
   - Meaning: nature and surroundings.
   - Natural: Environmental problems affect health and daily life.
   - IT/pro: Data centers and devices also have environmental costs.
   - Mistake: Avoid `environment pollution`; say `environmental pollution`.

6. **sustainability / sustainable / sustain**
   - Meaning: ability to continue without causing serious damage.
   - Natural: Sustainable habits are easier when they are convenient.
   - IT/pro: Sustainable engineering includes efficiency, cost, and long-term maintenance.
   - Mistake: Avoid `sustainability life`; say `a sustainable lifestyle`.

7. **impact / impact / impactful**
   - Meaning: effect or influence.
   - Natural: Small actions can have impact when many people do them.
   - IT/pro: A small design choice can impact millions of users.
   - Mistake: Avoid `make impact to`; say `have an impact on`.

8. **influence / influence / influential**
   - Meaning: power to change people's behavior or thinking.
   - Natural: Social media can influence what people believe.
   - IT/pro: Algorithms can influence what users see, buy, or trust.
   - Mistake: Avoid `influence to people`; say `influence people`.

9. **transparency / transparent / transparently**
   - Meaning: openness about how something works.
   - Natural: Transparency builds trust.
   - IT/pro: Users should know how their data is collected and used.
   - Mistake: Avoid `transparent for user`; say `transparent with users`.

10. **waste / wasteful / wastefully**
    - Meaning: using more than necessary.
    - Natural: Food waste and plastic waste are common problems.
    - IT/pro: Inefficient software can waste computing resources and energy.
    - Mistake: Avoid `many waste`; say `a lot of waste`.

### 2e. Common Vietnamese Learner Mistakes

```text
Mistake: The environment is polluted by many trashes.
Better: The environment is polluted by a lot of waste. / There is too much waste.
Why: "Trash" is usually uncountable; "waste" is more natural here.
```

```text
Mistake: We need protect environment.
Better: We need to protect the environment.
Why: Use "need to + verb" and "the environment".
```

```text
Mistake: Company should responsibility.
Better: Companies should take responsibility.
Why: Use verb phrase with the noun.
```

```text
Mistake: My private is leaked.
Better: My private information is leaked. / My privacy is violated.
Why: "Private" is an adjective; "privacy" is the noun.
```

```text
Mistake: Technology has bad affect to society.
Better: Technology has a bad effect on society. / Technology affects society.
Why: "Effect" is noun; "affect" is verb.
```

```text
Mistake: We should awareness people.
Better: We should raise people's awareness.
Why: Use "raise awareness".
```

```text
Mistake: It impacts to users.
Better: It has an impact on users. / It impacts users.
Why: "Impact" as verb does not need "to".
```

```text
Mistake: The app collect many data.
Better: The app collects a lot of data.
Why: Third-person singular and "data" as mass noun in spoken English.
```

## Part 3 - Core Idea Bank

### Idea 1: Responsibility Is Shared

Simple version:

```text
Everyone has some responsibility.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I think social and environmental problems need shared responsibility. Individuals should make better choices, but companies and governments also have more power to create real change.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
Users can protect their privacy, but companies should not design confusing settings or collect unnecessary data.
```

Useful language:

```text
shared responsibility / real change / unnecessary data
```

### Idea 2: Convenience Often Has A Hidden Cost

Simple version:

```text
Convenient things can still cause problems.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Modern life is very convenient, but convenience often has a hidden cost. Fast delivery, cheap products, and free apps may create waste, privacy risks, or pressure on workers.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
A free app may feel convenient, but users may pay with personal data and attention.
```

Useful language:

```text
hidden cost / privacy risks / balance convenience and responsibility
```

### Idea 3: Privacy Is About Control

Simple version:

```text
Privacy means controlling personal information.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
For me, privacy is not about hiding something bad. It is about having control over personal information and knowing how that information is used.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
In product design, privacy should be considered early, not added only after users complain.
```

Useful language:

```text
control over personal information / use data responsibly / privacy-aware design
```

### Idea 4: Algorithms Can Shape Behavior

Simple version:

```text
Algorithms affect what people see.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Algorithms can shape behavior because they decide what content people see repeatedly. This can be useful for personalization, but it can also narrow people's view of the world.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
A recommendation system may increase engagement, but it may also push users toward addictive or extreme content.
```

Useful language:

```text
influence user behavior / personalization / narrow people's view
```

### Idea 5: Sustainability Should Be Easy To Practice

Simple version:

```text
People are more sustainable when it is convenient.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I think sustainable choices should be made easier for normal people. If being environmentally friendly is too expensive or inconvenient, many people will not keep doing it.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
In software, efficient defaults can help teams save resources without forcing every developer to think about energy all the time.
```

Useful language:

```text
sustainable choices / easy to practice / efficient defaults
```

### Idea 6: Awareness Is Not Enough

Simple version:

```text
Knowing a problem is not enough.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Public awareness is important, but awareness alone does not solve problems. People also need clear systems, affordable choices, and rules that make responsible behavior easier.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
Users may care about privacy, but if privacy settings are confusing, awareness will not help much.
```

Useful language:

```text
raise awareness / clear systems / responsible behavior
```

### Idea 7: Companies Should Think Beyond Short-term Profit

Simple version:

```text
Companies should not only think about money.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Companies need profit to survive, but they should also think beyond short-term profit. If a product harms users, society, or the environment, the long-term cost can be much higher.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
Dark patterns may increase conversion in the short term, but they damage trust over time.
```

Useful language:

```text
short-term profit / long-term cost / damage trust
```

### Idea 8: Practical Ethics Is Better Than Perfect Ethics

Simple version:

```text
Ethics should be practical.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I think ethical thinking should be practical. Instead of giving perfect speeches, people and companies should ask simple questions: who may be harmed, what data is needed, and what trade-off is acceptable?
```

IT/professional example:

```text
As an engineer, I may not control every business decision, but I can still raise concerns about privacy, misleading design, or unnecessary complexity.
```

Useful language:

```text
practical ethics / who may be harmed / acceptable trade-off
```

## Part 4 - Speaking Patterns, Grammar & Expression Tips

### 4a. Sentence Patterns

1. **I think this issue matters because...**
2. **The responsibility should not fall only on...**
3. **The hidden cost is that...**
4. **From a technology point of view,...**
5. **A practical solution would be to...**

### 4b. Grammar Points To Review

#### Grammar 1: Passive Voice For Social Issues

```text
Personal data is collected by many apps.
The environment is affected by waste and pollution.
```

#### Grammar 2: Modal Verbs For Responsibility

```text
Companies should be transparent with users.
Governments need to create clear rules.
```

#### Grammar 3: Cause And Effect

```text
If apps collect too much data, users may lose trust.
When sustainable choices are convenient, more people follow them.
```

#### Grammar 4: "Not Only... But Also..."

```text
Privacy is not only a personal issue, but also a product design issue.
Sustainability requires not only awareness, but also better systems.
```

#### Grammar 5: Hedging For Abstract Topics

```text
I would say this depends on the context.
This can be useful, but it may also create risks.
```

### 4c. Answer Patterns

```text
Issue -> why it matters -> practical example -> balanced view
Privacy matters because people should control their personal information. For example, apps should collect only necessary data. Of course, data can improve services, but it should be used responsibly.
```

```text
Problem -> hidden cost -> responsibility
Convenience is useful, but it can have hidden costs. That is why responsibility should be shared between individuals, companies, and governments.
```

### 4d. Expression Tips

- Say **the environment**, not just **environment**.
- Use **privacy**, not **private**, when talking about the concept.
- Say **have an impact on**, not **impact to**.
- Use **ethical issue** instead of **ethic problem**.
- Keep abstract answers practical by adding one technology or daily-life example.

### 4e. Upgrade Pattern

```text
Basic: Environment is important and people should protect it.
Better: The environment matters because pollution and waste affect people's health and daily life.
Strong: The environment matters because pollution, waste, and energy use affect people's health, daily life, and future choices, so responsibility should be shared by individuals, companies, and governments.
```

### 4f. Repair Strategies

```text
If the topic feels too abstract:
Start with a concrete example: "For example, many apps collect user data..."
```

```text
If you forget "sustainable":
"I mean something people can continue for a long time without causing damage..."
Then say: "sustainable."
```

```text
If your answer sounds like an essay:
Add personal context: "As someone working in technology, I notice that..."
```

## Part 5 - Guided Speaking Practice

### 5a. IELTS Speaking Part 1 Practice

#### Question 1: Do you care about environmental problems?

```text
Yes, I do, although I am not perfect. I try to reduce waste and make small sustainable choices in daily life.
```

#### Question 2: Is privacy important to you?

```text
Yes, privacy is important because personal data can reveal a lot about a person. I prefer apps that are transparent about how they use data.
```

#### Question 3: Do you think technology affects society?

```text
Definitely. Technology affects how people work, communicate, learn, and even make decisions, so it has a large social impact.
```

#### Question 4: What can people do to protect the environment?

```text
People can reduce waste, save energy, and make more sustainable choices. But I also think companies and governments need to help.
```

#### Question 5: Are companies responsible for social problems?

```text
Partly, yes. Companies do not cause every problem, but they should take responsibility when their products influence users or create hidden costs.
```

#### Question 6: Do you trust apps with your personal data?

```text
It depends on the app. I trust companies more when they collect only necessary data and explain their privacy policy clearly.
```

### 5b. IELTS Speaking Part 2 Cue Card

Describe an issue in society that you think is important.

You should say:

- what the issue is
- why it matters
- who is affected by it
- and explain what people or organizations should do about it

### Model Answer

```text
An issue I think is important is digital privacy. It matters because people use apps for almost everything now: communication, banking, learning, shopping, and work. As a result, companies can collect a lot of personal data.

For me, privacy is not about hiding something bad. It is about control. Users should know what data is collected, why it is needed, and how it will be used. If companies collect too much data or use it unclearly, people may lose trust.

This issue affects almost everyone, but many people do not fully understand it because privacy settings and policies are often confusing. From a technology point of view, I think privacy should be considered early in product design, not added at the end.

A practical solution would be for companies to collect only necessary data, explain choices clearly, and avoid harmful design patterns. Governments can also create clearer rules. Individual users should be careful too, but responsibility should not fall only on them.
```

### Shadowing Version

```text
Privacy is about control.
Users should know what data is collected.
Companies should collect only necessary data.
Privacy should be considered early in product design.
Responsibility should not fall only on users.
```

### 5c. IELTS Speaking Part 3 Practice

#### Question 1: Who should be responsible for environmental protection?

```text
I think responsibility should be shared. Individuals should reduce waste and save energy, but companies and governments have more power to change systems, prices, rules, and infrastructure.
```

#### Question 2: Should companies be punished for harming the environment?

```text
Yes, if the harm is serious or careless. Without clear consequences, some companies may choose short-term profit over long-term responsibility.
```

#### Question 3: Is technology good or bad for society?

```text
It is both. Technology can improve education, health, and communication, but it can also create privacy risks, addiction, and inequality if it is designed irresponsibly.
```

#### Question 4: Why do people ignore environmental problems?

```text
Some people ignore them because the problems feel distant or too big. Also, sustainable choices are not always convenient or affordable, so awareness alone is not enough.
```

#### Question 5: Should governments regulate technology companies more strictly?

```text
In some areas, yes, especially privacy, data use, and harmful design patterns. But regulation should be clear and practical, so it protects users without blocking useful innovation.
```
