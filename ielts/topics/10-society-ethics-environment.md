## Part 2 - Vocabulary In Context

### 2a. Word Families

1. **society / social / socially**
   - Meaning: people living together and shared systems.
   - Natural: Social problems usually need both personal action and public support.
   - IT/pro: Technology products can influence society at a large scale.
   - Mistake: Avoid `in the society`; say `in society` or `in modern society`.

2. **responsibility / responsible / responsibly**
   - Meaning: duty to act carefully.
   - Natural: Everyone has some responsibility for the environment.
   - IT/pro: Engineers should build products responsibly, especially when user data is involved.
   - Mistake: Avoid `take responsibility about`; say `take responsibility for`.

3. **ethics / ethical / ethically**
   - Meaning: ideas about what's right or fair.
   - Natural: Ethical decisions aren't always simple.
   - IT/pro: Using personal data ethically is a major issue in digital products.
   - Mistake: Avoid `ethic problem`; say `ethical problem` or `ethical issue`.

4. **privacy / private / privately**
   - Meaning: control over personal information.
   - Natural: Privacy matters because people should control their own data.
   - IT/pro: Apps should collect only the data they really need.
   - Mistake: Avoid `my private is important`; say `my privacy is important`.

5. **environment / environmental / environmentally**
   - Meaning: nature and surroundings.
   - Natural: Environmental problems affect health and daily life.
   - IT/pro: Data centers and devices also have environmental costs.
   - Mistake: Avoid `environment pollution`; say `environmental pollution`.

6. **sustainability / sustainable / sustain**
   - Meaning: ability to continue without causing serious damage.
   - Natural: Sustainable habits are easier when they're convenient.
   - IT/pro: Sustainable engineering includes efficiency, cost, and long-term maintenance.
   - Mistake: Avoid `sustainability life`; say `a sustainable lifestyle`.

7. **impact / impact / impactful**
   - Meaning: effect or influence.
   - Natural: Small actions can have impact when many people do them.
   - IT/pro: A small design choice can impact millions of users.
   - Mistake: Avoid `make impact to`; say `have an impact on`.

8. **influence / influence / influential**
   - Meaning: power to change people's behavior or thinking.
   - Natural: Social media can influence what people believe.
   - IT/pro: Algorithms can influence what users see, buy, or trust.
   - Mistake: Avoid `influence to people`; say `influence people`.

9. **transparency / transparent / transparently**
   - Meaning: openness about how something works.
   - Natural: Transparency builds trust.
   - IT/pro: Users should know how their data is collected and used.
   - Mistake: Avoid `transparent for user`; say `transparent with users`.

10. **waste / wasteful / wastefully**
    - Meaning: using more than necessary.
    - Natural: Food waste and plastic waste are common problems.
    - IT/pro: Inefficient software can waste computing resources and energy.
    - Mistake: Avoid `many waste`; say `a lot of waste`.

### 2e. Common Vietnamese Learner Mistakes

```text
Mistake: The environment is polluted by many trashes.
Better: The environment is polluted by a lot of waste. / There's too much waste.
Why: "Trash" is usually uncountable; "waste" is more natural here.
```

```text
Mistake: We need protect environment.
Better: We need to protect the environment.
Why: Use "need to + verb" and "the environment".
```

```text
Mistake: Company should responsibility.
Better: Companies should take responsibility.
Why: Use verb phrase with the noun.
```

```text
Mistake: My private is leaked.
Better: My private information is leaked. / My privacy is violated.
Why: "Private" is an adjective; "privacy" is the noun.
```

```text
Mistake: Technology has bad affect to society.
Better: Technology has a bad effect on society. / Technology affects society.
Why: "Effect" is noun; "affect" is verb.
```

```text
Mistake: We should awareness people.
Better: We should raise people's awareness.
Why: Use "raise awareness".
```

```text
Mistake: It impacts to users.
Better: It has an impact on users. / It impacts users.
Why: "Impact" as verb does not need "to".
```

```text
Mistake: The app collect many data.
Better: The app collects a lot of data.
Why: Third-person singular and "data" as mass noun in spoken English.
```

### 4c. Answer Patterns

```text
Issue -> why it matters -> practical example -> balanced view
Privacy matters because people should control their personal information. For example, apps should collect only necessary data. Of course, data can improve services, but it should be used responsibly.
```

```text
Problem -> hidden cost -> responsibility
Convenience is useful, but it can have hidden costs. That's why responsibility should be shared between individuals, companies, and governments.
```

### 4d. Expression Tips

- Say **the environment**, not just **environment**.
- Use **privacy**, not **private**, when talking about the concept.
- Say **have an impact on**, not **impact to**.
- Use **ethical issue** instead of **ethic problem**.
- Keep abstract answers practical by adding one technology or daily-life example.

### 4e. Upgrade Pattern

```text
Basic: Environment is important and people should protect it.
Better: The environment matters because pollution and waste affect people's health and daily life.
Strong: The environment matters because pollution, waste, and energy use affect people's health, daily life, and future choices, so responsibility should be shared by individuals, companies, and governments.
```

### 4f. Repair Strategies

```text
If the topic feels too abstract:
Start with a concrete example: "For example, many apps collect user data..."
```

```text
If you forget "sustainable":
"I mean something people can continue for a long time without causing damage..."
Then say: "sustainable."
```

```text
If your answer sounds like an essay:
Add personal context: "As someone working in technology, I notice that..."
```

### 5b. IELTS Speaking Part 2 Cue Card

Describe an issue in society that you think is important.

You should say:

- what the issue is
- why it matters
- who is affected by it
- and explain what people or organizations should do about it

### Model Answer

```text
An issue I think is important is digital privacy. It matters because people use apps for almost everything now: communication, banking, learning, shopping, and work. As a result, companies can collect a lot of personal data.

For me, privacy isn't about hiding something bad. It's about control. Users should know what data is collected, why it's needed, and how it will be used. If companies collect too much data or use it unclearly, people may lose trust.

This issue affects almost everyone, but many people don't fully understand it because privacy settings and policies are often confusing. From a technology point of view, I think privacy should be considered early in product design, not added at the end.

A practical solution would be for companies to collect only necessary data, explain choices clearly, and avoid harmful design patterns. Governments can also create clearer rules. Individual users should be careful too, but responsibility shouldn't fall only on them.
```

### Shadowing Version
