## Part 2 - Vocabulary In Context

### 2a. Word Families

1. **habit / habitual / build a habit**
   - Meaning: repeated behavior.
   - Natural: A small habit is easier to keep than a perfect plan.
   - IT/pro: I build a learning habit by reading docs and testing one small idea each day.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I make a habit learning`; say `I build a habit of learning`.

2. **routine / routinely / stick to a routine**
   - Meaning: regular structure.
   - Natural: A simple routine helps me study when motivation is low.
   - IT/pro: My routine includes coding practice, review, and English speaking.
   - Mistake: Avoid `do routine`; say `follow/stick to a routine`.

3. **motivation / motivate / motivated**
   - Meaning: reason or feeling that pushes action.
   - Natural: Motivation helps people start, but it doesn't always last.
   - IT/pro: Career goals motivate me to improve English and fullstack skills.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I have motivate`; say `I feel motivated`.

4. **consistency / consistent / consistently**
   - Meaning: regular effort over time.
   - Natural: Consistency matters more than studying hard for one day.
   - IT/pro: Consistent practice helps me become more confident in speaking and coding.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I need consistent`; say `I need consistency`.

5. **discipline / disciplined / self-discipline**
   - Meaning: ability to continue when it's not easy.
   - Natural: Discipline should support progress, not become self-punishment.
   - IT/pro: Self-discipline helps me learn after work even when I'm tired.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I'm discipline`; say `I'm disciplined`.

6. **progress / progress / progressive**
   - Meaning: improvement over time.
   - Natural: Slow progress is still progress if the direction is right.
   - IT/pro: I measure progress by clearer explanations and fewer repeated mistakes.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I have many progresses`; say `I have made progress`.

7. **improvement / improve / improved**
   - Meaning: becoming better.
   - Natural: Improvement is easier when the goal is specific.
   - IT/pro: I improve by recording answers, reviewing weak points, and retrying.
   - Mistake: Avoid `improve myself in English`; say `improve my English`.

8. **comfort zone / comfortable / uncomfortable**
   - Meaning: familiar area that feels safe.
   - Natural: Growth often begins when people leave their comfort zone gradually.
   - IT/pro: Speaking English in meetings pushes me outside my comfort zone.
   - Mistake: Avoid `out of comfort`; say `outside my comfort zone`.

9. **setback / set back / recover from a setback**
   - Meaning: problem that slows progress.
   - Natural: A setback doesn't mean the whole plan failed.
   - IT/pro: If I miss practice for a few days, I try to restart quickly.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I got setback`; say `I had a setback`.

10. **accountability / accountable / hold yourself accountable**
   - Meaning: support or responsibility that helps follow-through.
   - Natural: Accountability helps when motivation disappears.
   - IT/pro: Tracking lessons and recordings helps me stay accountable.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I need someone control me`; say `I need accountability`.

### 2e. Common Vietnamese Learner Mistakes

```text
Mistake: I learn English for improve myself.
Better: I learn English to improve my communication.
Why: Use "to + verb"; say the specific skill.
```

```text
Mistake: I very want to change.
Better: I really want to change.
Why: "Very" does not modify verbs.
```

```text
Mistake: I need more discipline myself.
Better: I need more self-discipline. / I need to be more disciplined.
Why: Use noun/adjective correctly.
```

```text
Mistake: I lack of motivation.
Better: I lack motivation. / I have a lack of motivation.
Why: "Lack" as a verb does not need "of".
```

```text
Mistake: I improve a lot of vocabularies.
Better: I improve my vocabulary.
Why: "Vocabulary" is usually uncountable.
```

```text
Mistake: I am boring when study.
Better: I get bored when I study.
Why: "Bored" describes your feeling.
```

```text
Mistake: I failed one day so I stop.
Better: I missed one day, so I stopped.
Why: Use past tense consistently.
```

```text
Mistake: I study by myself alone.
Better: I study by myself. / I study alone.
Why: Avoid repeating the same meaning.
```

## Part 4 - Speaking Patterns, Grammar & Expression Tips

### 4a. Sentence Patterns

1. **I'm trying to improve... because...**
   - I'm trying to improve my speaking because I need clearer professional communication.
   - Task: answer what skill you want to improve.

2. **The hard part isn't..., but...**
   - The hard part isn't starting, but staying consistent.
   - Task: explain why habits are difficult.

3. **I learn best when...**
   - I learn best when I can apply new ideas in a real project.
   - Task: describe your learning style.

4. **One thing that helps me is...**
   - One thing that helps me is recording short answers and retrying them.
   - Task: explain a self-study method.

5. **Even if I miss one day, I try to...**
   - Even if I miss one day, I try to restart quickly.
   - Task: talk about setbacks.

### 4b. Topic Grammar In Use

#### Grammar 1: Present Perfect For Progress

Function:

```text
Talk about improvement up to now.
```

Formula:

```text
I have improved...
I have learned how to...
```

Examples:

```text
I have improved my vocabulary by learning chunks.
I have learned how to explain my frontend work more clearly.
```

Upgrade:

```text
Basic: My English better.
Better: My English has improved because I practice speaking more regularly.
```

Drill:

```text
Use "I have improved..." to describe one skill.
```

#### Grammar 2: Cause And Effect

Function:

```text
Explain why habits succeed or fail.
```

Formula:

```text
Because of X, ...
This helps...
This can lead to...
```

Examples:

```text
Because of a simple routine, I can study even when I'm busy.
Too much pressure can lead to giving up.
```

Drill:

```text
Use "This helps..." to explain one learning habit.
```

#### Grammar 3: Contrast

Function:

```text
Balance motivation and discipline.
```

Formula:

```text
Although X, ...
X is useful, but...
```

Examples:

```text
Motivation is useful, but it's not reliable every day.
Although self-study is flexible, it requires discipline.
```

Drill:

```text
Use "Although..." to talk about self-study.
```

#### Grammar 4: Conditionals

Function:

```text
Talk about habits and results.
```

Formula:

```text
If I + present simple, I can...
If I didn't..., I'd...
```

Examples:

```text
If I start small, I can stay consistent.
If I didn't track my progress, I'd lose motivation more easily.
```

Drill:

```text
Use "If I..." to explain one routine.
```

#### Grammar 5: Gerunds As Subjects

Function:

```text
Talk about activities naturally.
```

Formula:

```text
Recording myself helps...
Learning in context makes...
```

Examples:

```text
Recording myself helps me notice repeated mistakes.
Learning vocabulary in chunks makes speaking easier.
```

Drill:

```text
Start an answer with a gerund: "Practicing..."
```
