## Part 2 - Vocabulary In Context

### 2a. Word Families

1. **priority / prioritize / high-priority**
   - Meaning: the most important thing.
   - Natural: When I am busy, I try to focus on the real priority.
   - IT/pro: I prioritize tasks based on user impact, deadlines, and risk.
   - Mistake: Avoid `the first priority task`; say `the top priority` or `the highest-priority task`.

2. **focus / focused / lose focus**
   - Meaning: attention on one thing.
   - Natural: I lose focus when I switch between too many tasks.
   - IT/pro: Deep work helps me stay focused when I am solving a complex bug.
   - Mistake: Avoid `concentrate in work`; say `concentrate on work`.

3. **distraction / distract / distracted**
   - Meaning: something that pulls attention away.
   - Natural: Notifications are a common distraction.
   - IT/pro: I mute chat apps when I need to review code carefully.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I am distraction`; say `I am distracted`.

4. **deadline / due / overdue**
   - Meaning: the time when something must be finished.
   - Natural: Deadlines help me move, but they can also create stress.
   - IT/pro: A clear deadline helps the team decide what to build first.
   - Mistake: Avoid `the deadline is expired`; say `the deadline has passed`.

5. **schedule / schedule / scheduled**
   - Meaning: a plan for time.
   - Natural: My schedule is not perfect, but it gives me structure.
   - IT/pro: I schedule focused blocks for coding and lighter blocks for meetings.
   - Mistake: Avoid `arrange my time table`; say `plan my schedule`.

6. **routine / routinely / daily routine**
   - Meaning: repeated structure.
   - Natural: A morning routine helps me start the day with less friction.
   - IT/pro: I routinely check priorities before opening Slack or email.
   - Mistake: Avoid `do a routine`; say `follow a routine`.

7. **procrastination / procrastinate / procrastinator**
   - Meaning: delaying important work.
   - Natural: I procrastinate when a task feels too big or unclear.
   - IT/pro: Breaking a feature into smaller tasks helps me avoid procrastination.
   - Mistake: Avoid `delay to do`; say `delay doing` or `put off doing`.

8. **efficiency / efficient / efficiently**
   - Meaning: using time and energy well.
   - Natural: Productivity is not only speed; it is also working efficiently.
   - IT/pro: Automation makes repetitive work more efficient.
   - Mistake: Avoid `work effective`; say `work efficiently`.

9. **energy / energetic / mental energy**
   - Meaning: physical or mental capacity.
   - Natural: I plan difficult tasks when I have more mental energy.
   - IT/pro: I avoid important architecture decisions when I am mentally tired.
   - Mistake: Avoid `my energy is lowly`; say `my energy is low`.

10. **balance / balanced / work-life balance**
    - Meaning: a healthy mix of responsibilities and rest.
    - Natural: A balanced routine is easier to keep long term.
    - IT/pro: Good productivity should protect work-life balance, not destroy it.
    - Mistake: Avoid `balance between work and life is good for me`; say `work-life balance is important to me`.

### 2e. Common Vietnamese Learner Mistakes

```text
Mistake: I have many works to do.
Better: I have a lot of work to do. / I have many tasks to finish.
Why: "Work" is usually uncountable.
```

```text
Mistake: I need focus in my job.
Better: I need to focus on my job.
Why: Use "focus on".
```

```text
Mistake: I spend two hours for coding.
Better: I spend two hours coding. / I spend two hours on coding.
Why: Use "spend time doing" or "spend time on something".
```

```text
Mistake: I am very pressure.
Better: I am under a lot of pressure.
Why: "Pressure" is a noun, not an adjective.
```

```text
Mistake: I delay to do important task.
Better: I delay doing an important task. / I put off an important task.
Why: "Delay" is followed by a noun or gerund.
```

```text
Mistake: My deadline is very hurry.
Better: My deadline is tight.
Why: Use "tight deadline".
```

```text
Mistake: I make schedule every morning.
Better: I make a schedule every morning. / I plan my day every morning.
Why: Add an article or use a natural verb phrase.
```

```text
Mistake: I cannot concentrate because many notifications.
Better: I cannot concentrate because of many notifications.
Why: Use "because of + noun".
```

## Part 3 - Core Idea Bank

### Idea 1: Productivity Is About Priority, Not Just Speed

Simple version:

```text
Being productive does not mean doing everything fast.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
For me, productivity is more about choosing the right task than working faster. If I spend the whole day on low-priority work, I may look busy but still make little real progress.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
In software work, fixing a small but risky bug can be more valuable than finishing several low-impact tasks.
```

Useful language:

```text
set clear priorities / real progress / low-impact work
```

### Idea 2: Deep Work Needs Protection

Simple version:

```text
Some tasks need quiet time.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I think focus has to be protected because modern work is full of messages, meetings, and small interruptions. For difficult tasks, even a short distraction can make me lose the whole flow.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
When I debug or design a frontend architecture, I usually need a block of quiet time to think through the details.
```

Useful language:

```text
protect deep work time / lose the flow / small interruptions
```

### Idea 3: A Realistic Schedule Beats A Perfect Schedule

Simple version:

```text
A simple plan is better than an unrealistic plan.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I used to make very ambitious plans, but now I prefer a realistic schedule with some buffer time. It is less exciting, but it is much easier to follow.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
If I estimate a feature, I include time for testing, review, and unexpected bugs.
```

Useful language:

```text
realistic schedule / buffer time / easier to follow
```

### Idea 4: Energy Management Matters

Simple version:

```text
I cannot do hard tasks when I am too tired.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Time management is important, but energy management is just as important. I try to do difficult work when my mind is clear and leave lighter tasks for later in the day.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
I prefer making technical decisions in the morning rather than at the end of a long day.
```

Useful language:

```text
mental energy / lighter tasks / my mind is clear
```

### Idea 5: Distractions Are Often Environmental

Simple version:

```text
Distractions are not only about weak discipline.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I do not think distraction is only a personal weakness. Many tools are designed to grab attention, so I need to control my environment instead of blaming myself all the time.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
I turn off non-urgent notifications when I need to review a pull request carefully.
```

Useful language:

```text
reduce distractions / control my environment / grab attention
```

### Idea 6: Saying No Is Part Of Time Management

Simple version:

```text
Sometimes I need to say no.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Time management is not only about calendars. It also includes saying no politely when I already have too much on my plate.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
If a request is not urgent, I may ask to move it to the next sprint instead of accepting everything immediately.
```

Useful language:

```text
say no politely / too much on my plate / avoid overcommitting
```

### Idea 7: Pressure Can Help, But Too Much Pressure Hurts

Simple version:

```text
Some pressure is useful, but too much is bad.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
A little pressure can help me focus, especially when there is a clear deadline. But if the pressure is constant, it becomes stressful and the quality of work usually drops.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
Before a release, pressure can push the team to finish, but constant urgency often leads to technical debt.
```

Useful language:

```text
work under pressure / clear deadline / quality drops
```

### Idea 8: Productivity Should Be Sustainable

Simple version:

```text
I want a routine I can keep.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
For me, a productive lifestyle should be sustainable. If a system only works when I sacrifice sleep, health, or relationships, it is not a good system in the long run.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
I would rather make steady progress for months than burn out after one intense week.
```

Useful language:

```text
sustainable workflow / steady progress / in the long run
```

## Part 4 - Speaking Patterns, Grammar & Expression Tips

### 4a. Sentence Patterns

1. **When I have many things to do, I usually...**
2. **I try to focus on..., rather than...**
3. **One thing that helps me stay productive is...**
4. **If the deadline is tight, I tend to...**
5. **It depends on whether the task is urgent or important.**

### 4b. Grammar Points To Review

#### Grammar 1: Time Clauses

```text
When I have many tasks, I set clear priorities first.
Before I start coding, I review what is most important.
```

#### Grammar 2: Contrast With "But" And "However"

```text
Deadlines can be useful, but too much pressure affects quality.
I like flexible schedules. However, I still need some structure.
```

#### Grammar 3: Conditionals For Work Habits

```text
If I am tired, I do lighter tasks.
If a task is complex, I block quiet time for it.
```

#### Grammar 4: "Used To" For Past Habits

```text
I used to check messages too often.
I used to make unrealistic plans, but now I leave buffer time.
```

#### Grammar 5: Gerunds After Common Verbs

```text
I avoid switching tasks too often.
I enjoy planning my day in the morning.
```

### 4c. Answer Patterns

```text
Direct answer -> reason -> example -> small contrast
Yes, I am fairly good at managing my time because I set priorities. For example, I usually do difficult work in the morning. However, I still get distracted by messages sometimes.
```

```text
Problem -> cause -> solution -> result
I used to lose focus because I checked notifications too often. Now I mute them during deep work, so it is easier to stay on track.
```

### 4d. Expression Tips

- Say **I have a lot on my plate** for being busy, but do not overuse it.
- Say **tight deadline**, not **hurry deadline**.
- Say **focus on one task**, not **focus one task**.
- Use **urgent** for time pressure and **important** for value.
- Use **sustainable** when talking about long-term habits.

### 4e. Upgrade Pattern

```text
Basic: I am busy, so I make a plan.
Better: When I have a lot on my plate, I try to set clear priorities and focus on one important task at a time.
Strong: When I have a lot on my plate, I separate urgent tasks from important ones, leave some buffer time, and avoid context switching so I can make steady progress.
```

### 4f. Repair Strategies

```text
If you forget a word:
"I mean the thing that takes my attention away..."
Then say: "a distraction."
```

```text
If your answer is too short:
Add one work example: "For example, when I debug a complex issue..."
```

```text
If you sound too absolute:
Add balance: "It depends on the type of task."
```

## Part 5 - Guided Speaking Practice

### 5a. IELTS Speaking Part 1 Practice

#### Question 1: Are you good at managing your time?

```text
I am fairly good at it, but I am not perfect. I usually set clear priorities in the morning, and that helps me stay on track during the day.
```

#### Question 2: What distracts you when you work or study?

```text
Notifications distract me the most, especially work messages. If I am doing deep work, I usually mute them for a while.
```

#### Question 3: Do you like planning your day?

```text
Yes, but I prefer a simple plan. If my schedule is too detailed, it becomes stressful and I do not follow it.
```

#### Question 4: Do you often work under pressure?

```text
Sometimes, especially close to a deadline. A little pressure helps me focus, but constant pressure can affect the quality of my work.
```

#### Question 5: What time of day are you most productive?

```text
I am usually most productive in the morning because my mind is clearer. I try to do complex tasks before I get tired.
```

#### Question 6: How do you avoid procrastination?

```text
I break a task into smaller steps. When the first step feels easy, it is much easier to start.
```

### 5b. IELTS Speaking Part 2 Cue Card

Describe a time when you had to manage your time well.

You should say:

- what you had to do
- why time management was important
- what you did to stay organized
- and explain how you felt about the result

### Model Answer

```text
One time I had to manage my time carefully was when I was working on a frontend feature while also preparing for English speaking practice. I had a lot on my plate, so I could not just work randomly.

First, I separated urgent tasks from important ones. For work, the priority was to finish the risky part of the feature and leave enough time for testing. For English, I kept the practice small: one recording, one review, and one retry.

What helped me most was blocking time. I protected two focus blocks for coding, muted notifications, and left lighter tasks for later. I also added buffer time because bugs usually take longer than expected.

In the end, the result was not perfect, but it was stable. I finished the key work and still kept my English routine. That experience reminded me that good time management is not about doing everything; it is about choosing what matters and making steady progress.
```

### Shadowing Version

```text
I had a lot on my plate.
I separated urgent tasks from important ones.
I protected two focus blocks.
I left buffer time for unexpected issues.
Good time management is about choosing what matters.
```

### 5c. IELTS Speaking Part 3 Practice

#### Question 1: Why do many people struggle with time management?

```text
I think many people struggle because they have too many inputs: messages, meetings, social media, and personal responsibilities. Also, some tasks are unclear, so people delay starting them. Good time management usually needs both clear priorities and a realistic schedule.
```

#### Question 2: Is technology helpful or harmful for productivity?

```text
It depends on how we use it. Technology can be very helpful for planning, automation, and communication. However, it can also create constant distractions if people check notifications all the time.
```

#### Question 3: Should companies care about employees' work-life balance?

```text
Yes, because sustainable productivity benefits both employees and companies. If people are always overloaded, they may work fast for a short time, but quality and motivation usually drop later.
```

#### Question 4: Are deadlines always useful?

```text
Not always. A clear deadline can help people focus, but an unrealistic deadline can create pressure and reduce quality. I think deadlines are useful only when the scope is also realistic.
```

#### Question 5: How can young people build better productivity habits?

```text
They can start with small habits, like planning the next day, reducing distractions, and reviewing progress. The key is to build a system that is easy enough to repeat, even on busy days.
```
