## Part 2 - Vocabulary In Context

### 2a. Word Families

1. **success / succeed / successful**
   - Meaning: achieving something meaningful.
   - Natural: Success means different things to different people.
   - IT/pro: For me, success includes building useful products and growing as an engineer.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I success in career`; say `I succeed in my career` or `I am successful in my career`.

2. **value / value / valuable**
   - Meaning: what matters or has worth.
   - Natural: Money is valuable, but it is not my only value.
   - IT/pro: I value learning, autonomy, and meaningful work.
   - Mistake: Avoid `my value is money`; say `one of my values is financial stability`.

3. **stability / stable / stabilize**
   - Meaning: security and low uncertainty.
   - Natural: Financial stability gives people more choices.
   - IT/pro: A stable job helps me plan my learning and long-term career.
   - Mistake: Avoid `stable life is success`; say `a stable life is part of success`.

4. **ambition / ambitious / ambitiously**
   - Meaning: desire to achieve more.
   - Natural: Ambition is healthy when it does not destroy your life.
   - IT/pro: I am ambitious about becoming a strong fullstack engineer.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I have ambitious`; say `I am ambitious` or `I have ambition`.

5. **pressure / pressurize / pressured**
   - Meaning: stress from expectations.
   - Natural: Social pressure can make people chase goals they do not really want.
   - IT/pro: Career pressure can push developers to learn everything at once.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I am pressure`; say `I feel pressured`.

6. **expectation / expect / expected**
   - Meaning: what others think should happen.
   - Natural: Family expectations can influence career choices.
   - IT/pro: Some families expect stable jobs more than risky career moves.
   - Mistake: Avoid `my parents expect me success`; say `my parents expect me to be successful`.

7. **wealth / wealthy / wealthier**
   - Meaning: having a lot of money or assets.
   - Natural: Wealth can make life easier, but it does not solve every problem.
   - IT/pro: A high salary is useful, but it is not the only reason to choose a tech path.
   - Mistake: Avoid `rich is success`; say `being wealthy can be one form of success`.

8. **independence / independent / independently**
   - Meaning: ability to make choices without depending too much on others.
   - Natural: Financial independence gives people freedom.
   - IT/pro: Career independence lets me choose better projects and learning paths.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I want independent`; say `I want to be independent`.

9. **fulfillment / fulfill / fulfilling**
   - Meaning: feeling that life or work is meaningful.
   - Natural: A fulfilling job gives more than just money.
   - IT/pro: I find frontend fulfilling because it connects technical work with user experience.
   - Mistake: Avoid `my job is fulfillment`; say `my job is fulfilling`.

10. **trade-off / trade off / trade-offs**
    - Meaning: giving up one thing to gain another.
    - Natural: Every career choice has trade-offs.
    - IT/pro: A higher salary may come with longer hours or less learning time.
    - Mistake: Avoid `tradeoff between money with time`; say `trade-off between money and time`.

### 2e. Common Vietnamese Learner Mistakes

```text
Mistake: I very want to success.
Better: I really want to succeed. / I really want to be successful.
Why: Use verb or adjective form correctly.
```

```text
Mistake: Money is not everything but it important.
Better: Money is not everything, but it is important.
Why: Add "is" before adjective.
```

```text
Mistake: I want have stable.
Better: I want to have stability. / I want to have a stable life.
Why: Use noun or adjective with noun.
```

```text
Mistake: My family want me have high salary.
Better: My family wants me to have a high salary.
Why: Third-person singular and "want someone to do something".
```

```text
Mistake: I compare with other people.
Better: I compare myself with other people.
Why: Add object after "compare".
```

```text
Mistake: Success depend money.
Better: Success depends on money for some people.
Why: Use "depend on".
```

```text
Mistake: I choose this career because it has future.
Better: I chose this career because it has a future. / because it offers good opportunities.
Why: Use article or more natural phrase.
```

```text
Mistake: I don't want too material.
Better: I don't want to be too materialistic.
Why: Use adjective "materialistic".
```

## Part 3 - Core Idea Bank

### Idea 1: Success Needs A Personal Definition

Simple version:

```text
People should define success for themselves.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I think success needs a personal definition because people have different values, backgrounds, and responsibilities. If I only follow someone else's definition, I may achieve something that does not actually fit my life.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
For me, career success includes frontend mastery, fullstack growth, useful products, and enough freedom to choose better work.
```

Useful language:

```text
personal definition / fit my life / choose my own path
```

### Idea 2: Money Is Important, But Not Everything

Simple version:

```text
Money matters, but it is not the only thing.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I do not think it is realistic to say money is unimportant. Financial stability gives people safety and choices. But after a certain point, health, relationships, and meaningful work also matter a lot.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
In tech, a good salary is valuable, but a toxic environment can make even a high-paying job unsustainable.
```

Useful language:

```text
financial stability / safety and choices / meaningful work
```

### Idea 3: Social Comparison Creates Pressure

Simple version:

```text
People compare themselves too much.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Social comparison can create unnecessary pressure because people usually compare their private struggles with other people's public success. That can make them feel behind even when they are making progress.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
Developers may compare salaries, titles, or technologies, but everyone's path and timing are different.
```

Useful language:

```text
compare myself with others / feel behind / public success
```

### Idea 4: Family Expectations Often Come From Care

Simple version:

```text
Family pressure is often connected to love.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Family expectations can feel heavy, but I think they often come from care. Parents may want stability for their children because they have experienced uncertainty themselves.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
Some families may not fully understand tech careers, so they focus more on salary, company name, or job stability.
```

Useful language:

```text
come from care / job stability / feel heavy
```

### Idea 5: Ambition Should Be Sustainable

Simple version:

```text
Being ambitious is good, but health matters too.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I think ambition is positive when it gives people direction. But ambition becomes dangerous if it pushes people to sacrifice their health, relationships, or peace of mind for too long.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
I want to keep growing as an engineer, but I do not want to burn out by trying to learn everything at once.
```

Useful language:

```text
balance ambition and health / sacrifice personal time / sustainable growth
```

### Idea 6: Practical Choices Are Not Always Uninspiring

Simple version:

```text
Practical choices can still be meaningful.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Sometimes people talk as if practical choices are less meaningful, but I disagree. Choosing stability, income, or a clear career path can be responsible, especially when someone has family responsibilities.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
Choosing a stable frontend role can still support long-term goals like fullstack growth, cloud skills, and better communication.
```

Useful language:

```text
make a practical choice / family responsibilities / long-term goals
```

### Idea 7: Success Changes With Life Stages

Simple version:

```text
Success means different things at different ages.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I think the meaning of success changes over time. When people are younger, they may focus on achievement and recognition. Later, they may care more about stability, health, freedom, and relationships.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
At one stage, learning a new technology may feel like success; later, mentoring others or designing better systems may matter more.
```

Useful language:

```text
life stages / recognition / freedom and relationships
```

### Idea 8: Values Help With Trade-offs

Simple version:

```text
Values help people make decisions.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Personal values are useful because every path has trade-offs. If I know what matters most to me, it is easier to choose between money, time, learning, stability, and freedom.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
If a job pays more but gives me no growth or personal time, I need to decide whether that trade-off fits my current priorities.
```

Useful language:

```text
personal values / trade-offs / current priorities
```

## Part 4 - Speaking Patterns, Grammar & Expression Tips

### 4a. Sentence Patterns

1. **For me, success means...**
2. **I would not say money is everything, but...**
3. **One value I care about is...**
4. **Compared with the past, I now care more about...**
5. **The trade-off is that...**

### 4b. Grammar Points To Review

#### Grammar 1: Defining Abstract Ideas

```text
For me, success means having freedom, stability, and meaningful growth.
Success is not only about money; it is also about values.
```

#### Grammar 2: Concession With "Although" And "Even Though"

```text
Although money is important, it should not be the only goal.
Even though I am ambitious, I try to protect my health.
```

#### Grammar 3: Comparatives Over Time

```text
I care more about stability now than I did before.
I am less interested in external recognition than I used to be.
```

#### Grammar 4: Conditionals For Choices

```text
If a job gives me growth and stability, I would consider it successful.
If I only chase money, I may ignore my health.
```

#### Grammar 5: Noun Clauses With "What"

```text
What matters most to me is long-term growth.
What I value now is freedom and stability.
```

### 4c. Answer Patterns

```text
Definition -> contrast -> example -> personal view
For me, success means stability and growth. It is not only about money. For example, I want a career that supports my life and helps me improve. That feels more meaningful than chasing status.
```

```text
Balanced view -> realistic point -> value statement
Money is important because it gives people choices, but it should support life instead of controlling every decision.
```

### 4d. Expression Tips

- Say **financial stability**, not **stable money**.
- Use **materialistic** carefully; it can sound judgmental.
- Say **I feel pressured**, not **I am pressure**.
- Use **choose my own path** for personal direction.
- Use **trade-off** when talking about salary, time, health, and growth.

### 4e. Upgrade Pattern

```text
Basic: Success is money and happy.
Better: For me, success means financial stability, personal growth, and a life that feels meaningful.
Strong: For me, success means having enough financial stability to make choices, enough growth to feel challenged, and enough balance to protect my health and relationships.
```

### 4f. Repair Strategies

```text
If you forget "financial stability":
"I mean having enough money to feel safe and plan ahead..."
Then say: "financial stability."
```

```text
If your answer sounds too materialistic:
Add values: "Money matters, but it is not the only thing I care about."
```

```text
If your answer sounds too idealistic:
Add realism: "Of course, practical needs like income and stability still matter."
```

## Part 5 - Guided Speaking Practice

### 5a. IELTS Speaking Part 1 Practice

#### Question 1: What does success mean to you?

```text
For me, success means financial stability, growth, and enough freedom to choose a life that fits my values.
```

#### Question 2: Is money important to you?

```text
Yes, of course. Money is not everything, but financial stability gives me safety and more choices.
```

#### Question 3: Are you an ambitious person?

```text
I think I am, but in a practical way. I want to grow in my career, but I also want that growth to be sustainable.
```

#### Question 4: Do young people feel pressure to succeed?

```text
Yes, many young people feel pressure from family, social media, and comparison with others. It can make them feel behind even when they are progressing.
```

#### Question 5: Do you compare yourself with others?

```text
Sometimes, but I try not to do it too much. Everyone has a different background, timeline, and set of responsibilities.
```

#### Question 6: Would you choose a job only for money?

```text
Probably not only for money. Salary matters, but I would also consider growth, work culture, and personal time.
```

### 5b. IELTS Speaking Part 2 Cue Card

Describe a goal that is important to your future success.

You should say:

- what the goal is
- why it matters to you
- what you are doing to achieve it
- and explain how this goal connects to your values

### Model Answer

```text
An important goal for my future success is to become a stronger fullstack engineer, starting with mastering frontend first. This goal matters to me because frontend is close to users, and I enjoy seeing how a product becomes clearer, faster, and more useful through good interface work.

At the same time, I do not want to stay only in one narrow area forever. After working in frontend for several years, I have gradually expanded into backend, AWS cloud, AI tools, and DevOps. For me, that kind of growth creates more freedom and better problem-solving ability.

I am working toward this goal by studying consistently, building real projects, improving my English communication, and learning how to explain technical decisions more clearly.

This goal connects to my values because I care about growth, practical impact, and independence. Of course, financial stability is also important, but I do not define success only by salary. I want a career that gives me stability, meaningful work, and enough freedom to choose my own path.
```

### Shadowing Version

```text
I want to become a stronger fullstack engineer.
Frontend is close to users.
I care about growth, practical impact, and independence.
Financial stability is important, but it is not my only value.
I want enough freedom to choose my own path.
```

### 5c. IELTS Speaking Part 3 Practice

#### Question 1: Why do people define success differently?

```text
People define success differently because they have different values, backgrounds, and responsibilities. For one person, success may mean wealth; for another, it may mean freedom, health, or family stability.
```

#### Question 2: Is money the best measure of success?

```text
Not really. Money is an important measure because it affects quality of life, but it is not complete. A person can earn a lot and still feel unhealthy, lonely, or unfulfilled.
```

#### Question 3: How does social media affect people's idea of success?

```text
Social media often shows public success, not private struggle. This can make people compare themselves too much and feel behind, even when their own progress is normal.
```

#### Question 4: Should parents push children to become successful?

```text
They can encourage children, but pushing too hard may create pressure. I think support works better when parents understand the child's interests and values, not only status or income.
```

#### Question 5: Can ambition be harmful?

```text
Yes, if ambition becomes endless pressure. Ambition is useful when it gives direction, but it can be harmful if people sacrifice health, relationships, and personal values for too long.
```
