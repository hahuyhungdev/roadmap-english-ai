## Part 2 - Vocabulary In Context

### 2a. Word Families

1. **use / use / useful**
   - Meaning: help you do something.
   - Natural: My laptop is probably the most useful thing I own.
   - IT/pro: A reliable laptop is essential for coding, meetings, and learning.
   - Mistake: Avoid `it has useful`; say `it's useful`.

2. **practice / practicality / practical**
   - Meaning: useful in real life.
   - Natural: I prefer practical gifts that I can use regularly.
   - IT/pro: Practical tools matter more to me than expensive-looking accessories.
   - Mistake: Avoid `practice gift`; say `practical gift`.

3. **mean / meaning / meaningful**
   - Meaning: emotionally important.
   - Natural: A small gift can be meaningful if it shows care.
   - IT/pro: A notebook from my first job is meaningful because it reminds me of that period.
   - Mistake: Avoid `it is meaning for me`; say `it's meaningful to me`.

4. **own / ownership / owner**
   - Meaning: have something as yours.
   - Natural: I don't own many expensive things.
   - IT/pro: I own a few devices that I use for work and self-study.
   - Mistake: Avoid `I have own`; say `I own`.

5. **value / value / valuable**
   - Meaning: worth money or worth keeping.
   - Natural: Something can be valuable because of memory, not price.
   - IT/pro: My old keyboard isn't expensive, but it's valuable because I used it during many projects.
   - Mistake: Avoid `it is value`; say `it's valuable`.

6. **remind / reminder / memorable**
   - Meaning: make you remember something.
   - Natural: This watch reminds me of my father.
   - IT/pro: My first company card reminds me of the start of my career.
   - Mistake: Avoid `it remembers me`; say `it reminds me`.

7. **choose / choice / chosen**
   - Meaning: decide what to take or buy.
   - Natural: I choose gifts based on the person's lifestyle.
   - IT/pro: When buying devices, I choose based on reliability, not only brand.
   - Mistake: Avoid `choose depend price`; say `choose based on price`.

8. **last / longevity / long-lasting**
   - Meaning: continue for a long time.
   - Natural: I like long-lasting products because they save money over time.
   - IT/pro: A durable backpack is useful when I carry a laptop every day.
   - Mistake: Avoid `use in long time`; say `use for a long time`.

### 2e. Common Vietnamese Learner Mistakes

```text
Mistake: My friend gifted me a book.
Better: My friend gave me a book as a gift.
Why: "Gifted me" is possible in some contexts, but "gave me" is more natural for IELTS speaking.
```

```text
Mistake: It is very meaning with me.
Better: It's very meaningful to me.
Why: Use adjective "meaningful" and preposition "to".
```

```text
Mistake: This thing reminds me about my childhood.
Better: This thing reminds me of my childhood.
Why: Use "remind someone of something".
```

```text
Mistake: I use it everyday for working.
Better: I use it every day for work.
Why: "Every day" is two words as an adverbial phrase; say "for work".
```

```text
Mistake: It has high quality.
Better: It has good build quality. / It's high-quality.
Why: "Good build quality" sounds natural for objects.
```

```text
Mistake: I bought it by online.
Better: I bought it online.
Why: No "by" before online.
```

```text
Mistake: It costs expensive.
Better: It's expensive. / It costs a lot.
Why: "Cost" takes a price or amount, not adjective "expensive".
```

```text
Mistake: I very like this gift.
Better: I really like this gift.
Why: Use "really" before verbs, not "very".
```


## Part 4 - Speaking Patterns, Grammar & Expression Tips

### Pattern 1: Object And Purpose

```text
It's a small notebook that I use to write down ideas and reminders.
```

Use it for:

```text
It's a + object + that I use to...
```

### Pattern 2: Meaning Through Memory

```text
It's meaningful to me because it reminds me of my first job.
```

Use it for:

```text
It's meaningful to me because it reminds me of...
```

### Pattern 3: Value Beyond Price

```text
It wasn't expensive, but it has sentimental value.
```

Use it for:

```text
It wasn't expensive, but...
```

### Pattern 4: Buying Decision

```text
Before I buy something expensive, I compare options and think about whether it fits my needs.
```

Use it for:

```text
Before I buy..., I...
```

### Grammar Focus 1: Relative Clauses

Use relative clauses to describe objects naturally.

```text
It's a backpack that I use every day.
It's a gift that my friend gave me when I graduated.
It's a tool that helps me work more comfortably.
```

Upgrade:

```text
Basic: I have a laptop. It is useful.
Better: I have a laptop that I use for coding, meetings, and online learning.
```

### Grammar Focus 2: Past Simple For Gift Stories

Use past simple for when you received or bought something.

```text
I received it from my sister.
I bought it when I started my first job.
My friend gave it to me when I moved to a new city.
```

Speaking drill:

```text
Use past simple to explain when you got an important object.
```

### Grammar Focus 3: Contrast

Use contrast to show value beyond price.

```text
It looks ordinary, but it's very meaningful to me.
It was expensive, but I use it every day, so it's worth the money.
```

Speaking drill:

```text
Use `but` to compare price and personal value.
```

### Answer Patterns

Part 1:

```text
Direct answer -> object/gift type -> reason
```

Part 2:

```text
What it is -> how you got it -> what it looks like -> how you use it -> why it matters
```

Part 3:

```text
General opinion -> reason -> example -> contrast -> conclusion
```

### Expression Tips

- Instead of `very important`, say `meaningful to me`, `has sentimental value`, or `reminds me of...`.
- Instead of `good quality`, say `good build quality` or `durable`.
- Instead of `cheap`, say `affordable` when you mean positive price.
- Explain use, memory, and value, not only color or size.

### Repair Strategies

```text
I can't describe the design in detail, but I can explain why it matters.
What I mean is, the object itself is simple, but the memory is important.
A simple example would be a notebook from school or work.
Let me put it another way: the value is emotional, not financial.
```

### Part 2 Cue Card

```text
Describe an object that is important to you.

You should say:
- what it is
- how you got it
- what you use it for
- and explain why it is important to you
```

### 1-minute Planning Bullets

```text
Object: laptop / notebook / watch / backpack
How: bought it / received it as a gift
Use: work, study, daily routine
Importance: practical value + memory or career period
```

### Model Answer

```text
I'd like to talk about my laptop, because it's probably the most practical object I own.

I bought it a few years ago when I needed a more reliable device for work and self-study. It wasn't cheap, so before buying it, I compared several options, checked reviews, and thought carefully about what I really needed.

I use it almost every day. As a developer, I use it for coding, meetings, reading documentation, and learning new tools. So for me, it's not just an electronic device. It's a work tool that supports most of my daily routine.

The reason it's important to me is partly practical and partly personal. Practically, it saves time because it's fast enough and reliable. Personally, it reminds me of a period when I became more serious about my career and learning. I spent many evenings studying and building small projects on it.

So even though it's just a laptop, it has real value for me. It's worth the money because I use it regularly, and it also reminds me of my growth.
```

### Part 3 Practice

#### Question 1

```text
Why do people keep old objects?
```

Model answer:

```text
People keep old objects because they carry memories. The object itself may not be useful anymore, but it can remind people of a relationship, a place, or a period in life. In that sense, the emotional value is more important than the practical value.
```

#### Question 2

```text
Are expensive gifts always better?
```

Model answer:

```text
No, not always. Expensive gifts can be impressive, but they don't always feel personal. A simple but thoughtful gift can be better if it matches the person's lifestyle or shows real attention.
```

#### Question 3

```text
Why do some people buy things they don't need?
```

Model answer:

```text
I think people sometimes buy unnecessary things because of advertising, social pressure, or emotion. Buying something new can create a short feeling of progress or reward. The problem is that this feeling doesn't always last.
```

#### Question 4

```text
Should people repair things or replace them?
```

Model answer:

```text
It depends on the item. If the object is durable and repair is affordable, repairing it is better for money and the environment. But if repair costs almost as much as replacement, or the item affects work reliability, replacing it can be reasonable.
```
