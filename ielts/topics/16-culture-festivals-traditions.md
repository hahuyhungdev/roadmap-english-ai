## Part 2 - Vocabulary In Context

### 2a. Word Families

1. **celebrate / celebration / celebratory**
   - Meaning: do something special for an event.
   - Natural: Vietnamese families celebrate Tet with food, visits, and family gatherings.
   - IT/pro: Some teams celebrate project milestones to build morale.
   - Mistake: Avoid `make a celebration Tet`; say `celebrate Tet`.

2. **tradition / traditional / traditionally**
   - Meaning: customs passed from the past.
   - Natural: Traditionally, people visit relatives during Tet.
   - IT/pro: Even workplace culture has traditions, like year-end parties or team dinners.
   - Mistake: Avoid `traditional culture` when you mean `tradition`; choose the right noun.

3. **custom / customary / customarily**
   - Meaning: a common cultural practice.
   - Natural: Giving lucky money is a common custom during Tet.
   - IT/pro: In some companies, it's customary to share a short update before a holiday break.
   - Mistake: Avoid confusing `custom` with `customer`.

4. **preserve / preservation / preserved**
   - Meaning: keep something from disappearing.
   - Natural: Families preserve traditions by teaching children what they mean.
   - IT/pro: Documentation helps preserve team knowledge when people leave.
   - Mistake: Avoid `protect tradition from lost`; say `preserve a tradition`.

5. **connect / connection / connected**
   - Meaning: bring people closer.
   - Natural: Festivals help people feel connected to family and community.
   - IT/pro: Team rituals can make remote workers feel more connected.
   - Mistake: Avoid `connect with culture` if you mean `feel connected to culture`.

6. **gather / gathering / gathered**
   - Meaning: meet in one place.
   - Natural: Family gatherings are a major part of Tet.
   - IT/pro: Company gatherings can help people build trust outside daily tasks.
   - Mistake: Avoid `family gather`; say `family gathering` or `families gather`.

7. **respect / respect / respectful**
   - Meaning: show care or honor.
   - Natural: Many traditions teach people to be respectful to older family members.
   - IT/pro: Respectful communication matters when teams have different backgrounds.
   - Mistake: Avoid `respect with elders`; say `respect elders`.

8. **change / change / changing**
   - Meaning: become different over time.
   - Natural: Some traditions are changing because modern life is busier.
   - IT/pro: Company culture changes when teams become more global or remote.
   - Mistake: Avoid `traditions are change`; say `traditions are changing`.

### 2e. Common Vietnamese Learner Mistakes

```text
Mistake: In Tet, people visit family.
Better: During Tet, people visit family.
Why: Use "during" for a festival period.
```

```text
Mistake: We make Tet holiday.
Better: We celebrate Tet.
Why: Use "celebrate" for festivals.
```

```text
Mistake: My family has many traditional.
Better: My family has many traditions.
Why: "Traditional" is an adjective; "traditions" is a noun.
```

```text
Mistake: Young people don't respect with old people.
Better: Young people don't always respect older people.
Why: "Respect" does not need "with".
```

```text
Mistake: Tet is very meaning.
Better: Tet is very meaningful.
Why: Use adjective "meaningful".
```

```text
Mistake: I return hometown.
Better: I return to my hometown.
Why: Use "return to".
```

```text
Mistake: Tradition is disappeared.
Better: The tradition is disappearing. / The tradition has disappeared.
Why: Use active progressive or present perfect.
```

```text
Mistake: People give lucky money for children.
Better: People give lucky money to children.
Why: Use "give something to someone".
```

### Reading Shadowing Checkpoint A: Why Festivals Matter

#### Mini Reading

```text
Festivals are not only about food, decorations, or days off. At their best, they bring people together. During Tet, many Vietnamese families gather, prepare traditional food, visit relatives, and remember where they come from.

For me, the most important part is connection. Modern life can make people busy and separate, especially when young people move to big cities for work. A festival gives families a reason to pause, return home, and keep shared memories alive.
```

### Micro Skill: Avoid Overgeneralizing Culture

Weak answer:

```text
Vietnamese people always respect traditions.
```

Stronger answer:

```text
Many Vietnamese people still respect traditions, but the way they celebrate is changing because modern life is busier and families may live farther apart.
```


## Part 4 - Speaking Patterns, Grammar & Expression Tips

### Pattern 1: Festival Description

```text
Tet is a public holiday when families gather, visit relatives, and prepare traditional food.
```

Use it for:

```text
X is a festival/holiday when...
```

### Pattern 2: Meaning Beyond Activity

```text
The meaning behind this tradition is respect for family and older generations.
```

Use it for:

```text
The meaning behind + noun + is...
```

### Pattern 3: Change Over Time

```text
Compared to the past, many people celebrate Tet in a simpler way now.
```

Use it for:

```text
Compared to the past, ... now.
```

### Pattern 4: Preserve And Adapt

```text
I think traditions should be preserved, but they should also adapt to modern life.
```

Use it for:

```text
X should be preserved, but...
```

### Grammar Focus 1: Relative Clauses With When

Use `when` to define festivals and occasions.

```text
Tet is a time when families gather.
A festival is an occasion when people celebrate shared values.
A family meal is a moment when different generations can reconnect.
```

Upgrade:

```text
Basic: Tet is important. Families meet.
Better: Tet is an important time when families meet and reconnect.
```

### Grammar Focus 2: Comparison Over Time

Use it to discuss changing traditions.

```text
Compared to the past, celebrations are simpler now.
People used to spend more time preparing food.
These days, many families buy some dishes instead.
```

Speaking drill:

```text
Compare Tet in the past and Tet today using `compared to the past`.
```

### Grammar Focus 3: Modal Verbs For Opinion

Use `should`, `need to`, and `don't have to` for Part 3.

```text
People should preserve meaningful traditions.
Traditions need to adapt to modern life.
A celebration doesn't have to be expensive to be meaningful.
```

Speaking drill:

```text
Use `should` and `don't have to` to answer: Should people keep traditions?
```

### Answer Patterns

Part 1:

```text
Direct answer -> example festival/custom -> personal feeling
```

Part 2:

```text
What the festival/tradition is -> when it happens -> what people do -> personal memory -> why it matters
```

Part 3:

```text
General opinion -> cultural reason -> modern change -> balanced conclusion
```

### Expression Tips

- Use `during Tet`, not `in Tet`.
- Use `family gathering`, `visit relatives`, and `prepare traditional food` for Tet answers.
- Use `keep the meaning alive` when talking about preserving traditions.
- Use `adapt to modern life` for balanced Part 3 answers.
- Avoid saying all young people hate tradition or all older people are traditional.

### Repair Strategies

```text
I don't know the exact history, but I can explain how people celebrate it.
What I mean is, the tradition matters because it brings people together.
A simple example would be giving lucky money during Tet.
Let me put it another way: the activity can change, but the meaning can stay.
```

### Part 2 Cue Card

```text
Describe a festival or tradition that is important in your country.

You should say:
- what it is
- when it happens
- what people usually do
- and explain why it is important
```

### 1-minute Planning Bullets

```text
Festival: Tet
When: Lunar New Year
Activities: clean house, cook food, visit relatives, lucky money
Meaning: family connection, new start, respect older generations
```

### Model Answer

```text
I'd like to talk about Tet, which is the Lunar New Year holiday and probably the most important festival in Vietnam.

It usually happens around late January or February, depending on the lunar calendar. Before Tet, many families clean the house, buy flowers, prepare traditional food, and get ready to visit relatives. During Tet, people often return to their hometowns, gather with family, give lucky money to children, and send good wishes to each other.

For me, the most important part of Tet is not the decoration or the food, although those things are nice. The real meaning is family connection. Modern life can be busy, and many people live far from their parents or grandparents. Tet gives people a reason to pause and come back.

I also think Tet helps preserve cultural identity. Children can learn family customs, like greeting older relatives respectfully or understanding why people prepare certain dishes. Of course, the way people celebrate is changing. Some families make it simpler now, and I think that's fine. The important thing is to keep the meaning alive.
```

### Part 3 Practice

#### Question 1

```text
Why are traditions important?
```

Model answer:

```text
Traditions are important because they connect people to family, history, and cultural identity. They create shared memories and give people a sense of belonging. Without traditions, life may become more convenient, but also more disconnected.
```

#### Question 2

```text
Should traditions change over time?
```

Model answer:

```text
Yes, I think they should change when necessary. If a tradition becomes too expensive, stressful, or unrealistic, people may give it up completely. A better approach is to preserve the meaning but adapt the form to modern life.
```

#### Question 3

```text
Do festivals bring generations together?
```

Model answer:

```text
Yes, they can. Festivals create situations where grandparents, parents, and children spend time together. Even if they don't agree on everything, shared meals and customs can make the family connection feel stronger.
```

#### Question 4

```text
Can globalization harm local culture?
```

Model answer:

```text
It can, but not always. Globalization can make young people more interested in international trends, so some local customs may become less visible. However, it can also encourage people to explain and share their culture with a wider audience.
```
