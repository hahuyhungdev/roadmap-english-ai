## Part 2 - Vocabulary In Context

### 2a. Word Families

1. **cook / cooking / cooked**
   - Meaning: prepare food.
   - Natural: I don't cook every day, but I can make simple meals when I have time.
   - IT/pro: After a long coding day, I prefer quick cooking or simple meal prep.
   - Mistake: Avoid `make cooking`; say `cook` or `do some cooking`.

2. **eat / eating / eater**
   - Meaning: have food.
   - Natural: I try to eat more balanced meals during the week.
   - IT/pro: When I'm busy with deadlines, my eating routine can become messy.
   - Mistake: Avoid `eat outside`; say `eat out`.

3. **nutrition / nutritious / nutrients**
   - Meaning: food value for health.
   - Natural: Nutritious food doesn't have to be complicated or expensive.
   - IT/pro: If I eat badly for several days, my focus and energy drop.
   - Mistake: Avoid `nutrition food`; say `nutritious food`.

4. **convenience / convenient / conveniently**
   - Meaning: easy and practical.
   - Natural: Food delivery is convenient, but I shouldn't rely on it too much.
   - IT/pro: Convenience matters when I have back-to-back meetings or urgent tasks.
   - Mistake: Avoid `it's convenience`; say `it's convenient`.

5. **deliver / delivery / delivered**
   - Meaning: bring something to you.
   - Natural: I use food delivery when I'm too tired to cook.
   - IT/pro: Delivery apps are useful, but they can make spending less visible.
   - Mistake: Avoid `order delivery food`; say `order food delivery` or `order food`.

6. **spend / spending / expense**
   - Meaning: use money.
   - Natural: I try to control my daily spending without being too strict.
   - IT/pro: Small daily purchases can add up if I don't track them.
   - Mistake: Avoid `cost money for food`; say `spend money on food`.

7. **value / valuable / value-for-money**
   - Meaning: worth the cost.
   - Natural: I don't always choose the cheapest option; I look for value for money.
   - IT/pro: I treat tools and devices as long-term value, not just price.
   - Mistake: Avoid `cheap price quality good`; say `good value for money`.

8. **consume / consumer / consumption**
   - Meaning: buy, use, or eat things.
   - Natural: Online shopping has changed how people consume things.
   - IT/pro: Digital products can encourage impulsive consumption through notifications and discounts.
   - Mistake: Avoid `consume shopping`; say `consumer habits` or `shopping habits`.

### 2e. Common Vietnamese Learner Mistakes

```text
Mistake: I usually eat outside.
Better: I usually eat out.
Why: "Eat out" means eat at restaurants or outside the home.
```

```text
Mistake: I very like street food.
Better: I really like street food.
Why: Use "really" before verbs, not "very".
```

```text
Mistake: The price is very cheap.
Better: It's very cheap. / The price is low.
Why: "Cheap" describes the item, not usually the price.
```

```text
Mistake: I buy online many things.
Better: I buy many things online.
Why: Put "online" after the object or after the verb phrase.
```

```text
Mistake: It costs expensive.
Better: It's expensive. / It costs a lot.
Why: Use adjective with "be" or cost phrase with amount.
```

```text
Mistake: I spend money for food.
Better: I spend money on food.
Why: Use "spend money on + noun".
```

```text
Mistake: I cook by myself.
Better: I cook for myself.
Why: "For myself" means you cook your own meals.
```

```text
Mistake: I order food in app.
Better: I order food on an app.
Why: Use "on an app".
```

## Part 4 - Speaking Patterns, Grammar & Expression Tips

### Pattern 1: Habit With Frequency

```text
I usually cook at home during the week, but I eat out on weekends.
```

Use it for:

```text
I usually..., but I sometimes...
```

### Pattern 2: Recent Change

```text
Recently, I've been trying to order less food and cook more simple meals.
```

Use it for:

```text
Recently, I've been trying to + verb...
```

### Pattern 3: Balanced View

```text
Delivery apps are convenient, but I shouldn't rely on them too much.
```

Use it for:

```text
X is convenient, but...
```

### Pattern 4: Money Without Sounding Materialistic

```text
I don't mind spending money on something useful, but I try to avoid unnecessary purchases.
```

Use it for:

```text
I don't mind spending money on..., but...
```

### Grammar Focus: Present Perfect Continuous For Recent Habits

Use this when a habit started recently and is still happening.

```text
I've been cooking more often recently.
I've been trying to reduce delivery food.
I've been comparing prices before buying tech products.
```

Common mistake:

```text
Avoid: I am try to cook more recently.
Better: I've been trying to cook more recently.
```

### Expression Tips

- Don't say only `delicious`; mention texture, price, convenience, or memory.
- For shopping, explain your decision process: need, price, review, quality, value.
- Use `eat out`, `order food`, and `cook at home` accurately.
- For money topics, sound balanced: practical, not cheap; thoughtful, not strict.
- If you don't like shopping, say what you do instead.

### Part 2 Cue Card

```text
Describe something useful you bought recently.

You should say:
- what it was
- where or how you bought it
- why you bought it
- and explain why it was useful
```

### Model Answer

```text
I'd like to talk about a pair of headphones I bought recently. I bought them online after comparing prices and reading reviews for a few days.

The reason I bought them was quite practical. I work as a developer, so I spend a lot of time in front of my laptop, and I often need to focus during coding or meetings. My old headphones were uncomfortable, and the microphone wasn't very clear, so they started to affect my daily work.

I didn't choose the cheapest option. I looked for something that was good value for money: comfortable, reliable, and good enough for calls. After using them for a while, I think it was a good decision. They help me focus better, join meetings more clearly, and reduce background noise.

So for me, it wasn't just a random purchase. It was a practical investment because I use it almost every day.
```
