### 2e. Common Vietnamese Learner Mistakes

```text
Mistake: I very depend on my phone.
Better: I depend on my phone a lot. / I rely heavily on my phone.
Why: "Very" does not modify verbs.
```

```text
Mistake: Technology bring many benefit.
Better: Technology brings many benefits.
Why: Third-person singular needs "brings"; "benefit" should be plural here.
```

```text
Mistake: I use AI for improve my code.
Better: I use AI to improve my code. / I use AI for improving my code.
Why: Use "to + verb" or "for + verb-ing".
```

```text
Mistake: AI can replace human in the future.
Better: AI can replace some human tasks in the future.
Why: Be specific. AI replaces tasks, not all humans.
```

```text
Mistake: I search information on internet.
Better: I search for information on the internet.
Why: Use "search for" and "the internet".
```

```text
Mistake: This app is convenience.
Better: This app is convenient. / This app brings convenience.
Why: "Convenient" is the adjective.
```

```text
Mistake: Privacy is not safe.
Better: My personal data is not safe. / The app does not protect privacy well.
Why: Privacy is a concept; data or users can be safe.
```

```text
Mistake: I trust AI answer.
Better: I trust AI answers only after I verify them.
Why: "Answer" is countable; add a stronger condition.
```

## Part 3 - Core Idea Bank

### Idea 1: Technology Is Part Of Daily Work

Simple version:

```text
I use technology every day for work and communication.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
I rely heavily on technology because most of my work depends on a laptop, digital tools, online documentation, and communication platforms.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
As a developer, I use GitHub, Slack, documentation sites, cloud dashboards, and AI tools as part of my daily workflow.
```

Useful language:

```text
rely heavily on / digital tools / daily workflow
```

### Idea 2: AI Is Useful, But It Needs Verification

Simple version:

```text
AI helps me work faster, but I need to check it.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
AI is useful for first drafts, examples, summaries, and debugging ideas, but I treat it as a draft until I can verify the output myself.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
If AI suggests code, I still check tests, local behavior, and whether the answer fits our project context.
```

Useful language:

```text
treat it as a draft / verify AI output / project context
```

### Idea 3: Technology Saves Time When The Task Is Clear

Simple version:

```text
Technology can help people finish tasks faster.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
Technology is most helpful when the task is clear and repetitive. It can reduce manual work and free people to focus on higher-value thinking.
```

IT/professional example:

```text
Automation is useful for formatting, testing, deployment checks, and repeated QA flows.
```

Useful language:


### Idea 4: Digital Life Can Be Distracting

Simple version:

```text
Technology can distract people.
```

Better spoken version:

```text
The main downside is that digital tools can become distracting, especially when people receive constant notifications or spend too much time on personalized feeds.
```


Simple version:


## Part 4 - Speaking Patterns, Grammar & Expression Tips

### 4a. Sentence Patterns

#### Pattern 1: I rely on X to...

Function:

```text
Explain how you use technology in daily life.
```

Examples:

```text
I rely on my laptop to work, study, and communicate with my team.
I rely on AI tools to draft ideas, but I still verify the final result.
```

Task:

```text
Answer: What technology do you use the most?
```

#### Pattern 2: One thing I like about X is that...

Function:

```text
Explain a benefit naturally.
```

Examples:

```text
One thing I like about online learning is that I can study at my own pace.
One thing I like about AI tools is that they reduce blank-page time.
```

Task:

```text
Use this pattern to talk about AI.
```

#### Pattern 3: The main downside is that...

Function:

```text
Talk about disadvantages without sounding too negative.
```

Examples:

```text
The main downside is that people can become too dependent on their phones.
The main downside is that AI can create false confidence if nobody checks it.
```

Task:

```text
Use this pattern to answer: Do people rely on technology too much?
```

#### Pattern 4: I would not say X is perfect, but...

Function:

```text
Give a balanced opinion.
```

Examples:

```text
I would not say AI is perfect, but it is useful when the task is clear.
I would not say social media is always harmful, but it can become distracting.
```

Task:

```text
Give a balanced opinion about technology.
```

#### Pattern 5: Compared to the past,...

Function:

```text
Explain changes over time.
```

Examples:

```text
Compared to the past, people can learn new skills much faster online.
Compared to the past, developers have more tools but also more things to verify.
```

Task:

```text
Answer: How has technology changed work?
```

### 4b. Topic Grammar In Use

#### Grammar 1: Tendency

Function:

```text
Talk about common habits without sounding absolute.
```

Formula:

```text
People tend to + verb...
People are likely to + verb...
```

Examples:

```text
People tend to check their phones many times a day.
Younger people are likely to use AI tools for learning and work.
```

IT example:

```text
Developers tend to use AI for drafts, examples, and debugging ideas.
```

Upgrade:

```text
Basic: People use phone too much.
Better: People tend to use their phones too much, especially when they are bored.
```

Drill:

```text
Use "tend to" to answer: Do people rely on technology too much?
```

#### Grammar 2: Cause And Effect

Function:

```text
Explain impact clearly.
```

Formula:

```text
Because of X, ...
This has made it easier to...
This can lead to...
```

Examples:

```text
Because of online learning, people can study from almost anywhere.
Too many notifications can lead to distraction and stress.
```

IT example:

```text
AI tools can reduce blank-page time, but they can also lead to quality risk if output is not reviewed.
```

Upgrade:

```text
Basic: AI is useful.
Better: AI is useful because it helps me draft ideas faster and start from something concrete.
```

Drill:

```text
Use "This can lead to..." to explain one risk of technology.
```

#### Grammar 3: Contrast

Function:

```text
Balance benefits and risks.
```

Formula:

```text
Although X is useful, ...
Having said that, ...
```

Examples:

```text
Although technology is convenient, it can make people less patient.
AI is useful for drafting. Having said that, I still need to verify the output.
```

IT example:

```text
Although AI can speed up coding, accountability still stays with the engineer.
```

Upgrade:

```text
Basic: AI is fast but dangerous.
Better: Although AI can make work faster, it becomes risky when people trust it without verification.
```

Drill:

```text
Use "Although..." to answer: Is AI good for workers?
```

#### Grammar 4: Relative Clause

Function:

```text
Describe apps, tools, and systems.
```

Formula:

```text
I use an app that...
It is a tool that allows me to...
```

Examples:

```text
I use an app that helps me manage tasks.
It is a tool that allows people to communicate remotely.
```

IT example:

```text
I use AI tools that help me draft tests and summarize documentation.
```

Upgrade:

```text
Basic: I use Notion. It helps me organize.
Better: I use Notion, which helps me organize notes, tasks, and project ideas.
```

Drill:

```text
Describe one app using "that" or "which".
```

#### Grammar 5: Conditional Type 2

Function:

```text
Talk about imagined situations.
```

Formula:

```text
If I did not have X, I would...
If companies used X more carefully, they could...
```

Examples:

```text
If I did not have my laptop, I would find it hard to work remotely.
If people set better boundaries, they could use technology more healthily.
```

IT example:

```text
If teams used AI without review, they would probably create more hidden bugs.
```

Upgrade:

```text
Basic: Without internet, I cannot work.
Better: If I did not have the internet, I would find it almost impossible to work effectively.
```

Drill:

```text
Use "If I did not have..." to talk about a technology you need.
```

### 4c. Answer Patterns

Part 1:

```text
Direct answer -> specific tool -> daily example -> small contrast
```

Part 2:

```text
What it is -> how you use it -> why it helps -> one risk -> final opinion
```

Part 3:

```text
General opinion -> benefit -> risk -> example -> balanced conclusion
```


```text

```

### 5b. Part 2 - Cue Card

Cue card:

```text
Describe a piece of technology or digital tool you find useful.

You should say:
- what it is
- how often you use it
- what you use it for
- and explain why you find it useful
```

#### 1-minute Planning Bullets

```text
Tool: laptop + AI coding/research tools
Frequency: almost every day
Uses: coding, documentation, meetings, learning, drafting
Benefit: saves time, reduces blank-page time, supports remote work
Risk: output needs verification, can be distracting
Final view: useful when used with judgment
```

#### 2-minute Model Answer

```text
A piece of technology I find very useful is my laptop, especially when I combine it with AI tools and online documentation. I use it almost every day, both for work and self-study.

For work, my laptop is basically the center of my workflow. I use it to write code, check documentation, join online meetings, manage tasks, and communicate with my team. Recently, AI tools have also become part of that workflow. I use them to draft ideas, summarize information, and explore possible solutions when I get stuck.

What I find useful is not just the speed, but the way technology helps me start from something concrete. For example, if I need to write a test case or understand a library option, AI can reduce blank-page time. However, I do not treat the output as final. I still need to verify it with local code, tests, or official documentation.

There are some downsides as well. Technology can be distracting, and AI can create false confidence if the answer sounds correct but is actually wrong. So I think the key is to use technology with boundaries and judgment.

Overall, I would say my laptop and digital tools make my work more flexible and efficient. They do not replace thinking, but they support it when I use them carefully.
```

```text

What I find useful is...
For example, ...
However, ...
Overall, ...
```

### 5c. Part 3 - Discussion

#### Question 1: Will AI replace human workers?

Framework:

```text
Balanced opinion -> tasks vs people -> example -> risk -> conclusion
```

Model answer:

```text
I think AI will replace some tasks, but not all human workers. Repetitive work, simple drafting, and basic data processing may become more automated. However, people are still needed for judgment, communication, responsibility, and understanding context. In software development, for example, AI can draft code, but an engineer still needs to verify it and own the final decision. So I see AI more as a tool that changes jobs than a tool that removes all jobs.
```

Simpler version:

```text
AI may replace some simple tasks, but humans are still needed for decisions, communication, and responsibility.
```

Retry prompt:

```text
Use "replace some tasks, but not all workers".
```

#### Question 2: What are the disadvantages of relying on technology?

Framework:

```text
General risk -> example -> effect -> balanced ending
```

Model answer:

```text
The biggest disadvantage is overdependence. If people rely on technology for every small task, they may lose patience, focus, or basic problem-solving skills. For example, constant notifications can make it hard to do deep work, and AI tools can make people accept answers without thinking. Having said that, the problem is not technology itself. The real issue is using it without boundaries.
```

Simpler version:

```text
The main problem is that people may depend on technology too much and lose focus or basic skills.
```

Retry prompt:

```text
Use "overdependence", "deep work", and "boundaries".
```

#### Question 3: Should companies collect user data?

Framework:

```text
Conditional answer -> useful data -> privacy risk -> fair rule
```

Model answer:

```text
I think companies can collect user data if the data clearly supports the product experience. For example, a map app needs location data to give directions. But companies should not collect unnecessary personal data just because it may be useful later. A fair rule is data minimization: collect only what is needed, explain it clearly, and give users real control.
```

Simpler version:

```text
Companies can collect data when it is necessary for the feature, but they should explain it and avoid collecting too much.
```

Retry prompt:

```text
Use "data minimization" and "real control".
```

#### Question 4: How has technology changed education?

Framework:

```text
Positive change -> example -> limitation -> conclusion
```

Model answer:

```text
Technology has made education more flexible and accessible. People can learn from online courses, videos, AI tutors, and digital communities without being in a traditional classroom. This is especially useful for self-study learners. The limitation is that online learning requires discipline, because there is no teacher watching your progress every day. So technology gives access, but learners still need a system.
```

Simpler version:

```text
Technology makes learning easier to access, but students still need discipline and a clear plan.
```

Retry prompt:

```text
Use "accessible", "self-study", and "requires discipline".
```

#### Question 5: Is technology making people more social or less social?

Framework:

```text
Both sides -> connection benefit -> shallow interaction risk -> conclusion
```

Model answer:

```text
I think it does both. Technology makes people more connected because they can message, call, and work with others from anywhere. But it can also make interaction shallower if people replace real conversation with quick reactions or scrolling. In my view, technology improves social life when it supports real communication, but it becomes a problem when it replaces it.
```

Simpler version:

```text
Technology helps people stay connected, but it can make communication less deep if people only scroll or react online.
```

Retry prompt:

```text
Use "connected", "shallower", and "supports real communication".
```
