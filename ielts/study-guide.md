# IELTS Lesson Study Guide

## Goal

Use each IELTS lesson to create spoken output, not passive reading. A lesson is effective only when you can record an answer, review it, and retry with better language.

Target result per lesson:

```text
1 baseline recording
1 guided answer recording
1 final retry recording after review
```

---

## Recommended Study Flow

### Step 1: Record Baseline First

Before studying the lesson, record the baseline task in Part 1.

Rules:

- Do not prepare too much.
- Speak for around 60 seconds.
- Notice the biggest blocker: ideas, vocabulary, grammar, pronunciation, or fluency.

Why:

```text
The baseline shows what you can really say before input. This makes progress visible when you retry later.
```

---

### Step 2: Study Vocabulary As Chunks

In Part 2, do not memorize isolated words first. Focus on reusable chunks and collocations.

Priority order:

```text
1. Collocations & chunks
2. Chunk expansion practice
3. Common Vietnamese learner mistakes
4. IT/professional-use phrases
5. Word families
```

How to practice:

- Read the natural sentence aloud.
- Read the IT/professional sentence aloud if relevant.
- Make one sentence about your real life or work.
- Save 10-15 chunks you can actually use in answers.

Good target:

```text
By the end of Part 2, you should have 10-15 usable phrases, not 50 passive words.
```

---

### Step 3: Retell The Idea Bank

In Part 3, do not only read the ideas. Turn each idea into speech.

For each idea:

```text
1. Read the simple version.
2. Read the better spoken version.
3. Close the text.
4. Retell the idea in 20-30 seconds using 1-2 lesson chunks.
```

Why:

```text
IELTS Speaking needs flexible ideas. Retelling trains you to explain, not memorize.
```

---

### Step 4: Shadow The Reading Checkpoints

For each Reading Shadowing Checkpoint:

```text
1. Read the mini reading once for meaning.
2. Answer the comprehension questions.
3. Find the language chunks.
4. Shadow the script line by line.
5. Retell the passage in 45-60 seconds.
```

Rules:

- Speak aloud, not silently.
- Repeat difficult lines 3-5 times.
- Keep contractions natural: I'm, it's, don't, I'd, I'll.
- Use the required chunks in the spoken retell.

Why:

```text
This section trains mouth movement, rhythm, and chunk recall before full IELTS answers.
```

---

### Step 5: Use Patterns Immediately

In Part 4, choose only the most useful patterns for the topic.

Recommended target:

```text
3-4 sentence patterns
2 grammar points
2 repair phrases
```

Do not study grammar like a textbook. Use it to improve answers.

Example:

```text
Weak: Technology is useful.
Better: Technology is useful because it helps me automate repetitive tasks and focus on higher-value work.
```

---

### Step 6: Record Guided IELTS Answers

In Part 5, record answers in this order:

```text
1. Part 1 short answers
2. Part 2 cue card
3. Part 3 discussion answers
```

For each answer:

```text
1. Speak once without looking at the model.
2. Read the model answer.
3. Pick 2-3 useful chunks.
4. Speak again in your own words.
```

Important:

```text
Do not copy the model answer exactly. Borrow structure and chunks, then personalize the answer.
```

---

### Step 7: Retry After 24 Hours

Part 6 is mandatory.

Retry tasks:

```text
Retry 1: Part 1 answer
Retry 2: Part 2 answer
Retry 3: Part 3 answer
Final retry prompt
```

Best timing:

```text
Do the final retry the next day, without looking at the model answer first.
```

Success signal:

```text
Your final retry should sound clearer, more specific, and more natural than your baseline.
```

---

## Suggested Schedule

### Option A: 2-day lesson

Day 1:

```text
Part 1 baseline
Part 2 vocabulary chunks
Part 3 idea bank + checkpoint A
```

Day 2:

```text
Checkpoint B/C
Part 4 patterns
Part 5 guided speaking
Part 6 retry
```

### Option B: 3-day lesson

Day 1:

```text
Part 1 + Part 2
```

Day 2:

```text
Part 3 + Part 4
```

Day 3:

```text
Part 5 + Part 6 final retry
```

## How To Use AI Support

Use AI as a speaking coach, not as an answer memorizer.

### Vocab Mode

Use when you need to learn or reuse vocabulary from the lesson.

Good prompts:

```text
Give me 10 chunks from this lesson that I can use in Part 1 answers.
Quiz me on the collocations from Part 2.
Correct my sentence using lesson vocabulary: ...
Give me IT-related examples for these chunks: ...
```

### Ideas Mode

Use when you cannot expand an answer.

Good prompts:

```text
Help me expand this idea into a 45-second answer: ...
Give me 3 angles for this Part 3 question: ...
Turn this simple idea into a better IELTS Speaking answer: ...
Give me a Part 2 story frame for this cue card.
```

### Sentences Mode

Use when your sentence sounds too simple or unnatural.

Good prompts:

```text
Upgrade these sentences to band 6.5 speaking English: ...
Correct my grammar but keep my meaning: ...
Make this answer more natural and spoken: ...
Give me 5 shadowing lines from my answer.
```

### Practice Mode

Use when you want active speaking tasks.

Good prompts:

```text
Ask me 5 Part 1 questions from this lesson.
Give me a Part 2 cue card and a 1-minute planning frame.
Ask me one Part 3 question, then wait for my answer.
```

---

## Minimum Completion Checklist

A lesson is complete when you have done these:

- Recorded the Part 1 baseline.
- Saved 10-15 usable chunks.
- Retold at least 6 core ideas.
- Completed all reading-shadowing checkpoints.
- Recorded one Part 2 answer.
- Answered at least 3 Part 3 questions.
- Completed the final retry after review.
