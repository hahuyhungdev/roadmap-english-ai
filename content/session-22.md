# Session 22: Ambition, Risk, and the Ethics of Move Fast

**Level:** B1-B2  
**Focus:** Discuss when fast execution is smart and when it becomes reckless.

<details open>
<summary><strong>1) Vocabulary </strong></summary>

- **move fast** (v phrase) - execute quickly with minimal delay  
  _Example:_ Move fast helped us launch early, but bugs increased.

- **reckless** (adj) - taking unsafe risks  
  _Example:_ Shipping without testing was reckless.

- **growth pressure** (n) - pressure to increase users/revenue quickly  
  _Example:_ Growth pressure pushed unrealistic deadlines.

- **quality compromise** (n) - lowering quality to save time  
  _Example:_ Too many quality compromises hurt retention later.

- **founder mindset** (n) - leadership style focused on speed and action  
  _Example:_ Founder mindset can be strong, but needs risk balance.

- **ethical boundary** (n) - limit of acceptable behavior  
  _Example:_ We crossed an ethical boundary by hiding known flaws.

- **responsible innovation** (n) - progress with risk control  
  _Example:_ Responsible innovation needs testing and transparency.

- **user trust** (n) - confidence users have in product and company  
  _Example:_ Repeated failures can damage user trust quickly.

- **short-term win** (n) - quick benefit now  
  _Example:_ We got a short-term win but created long-term debt.

- **long-term cost** (n) - future damage from current shortcuts  
  _Example:_ Long-term cost appeared as constant hotfixes.

**Additional useful terms:**
- **risk threshold** (n) - maximum risk a team accepts
- **release gate** (n) - required checks before launch
- **ethical review** (n) - discussion of possible harm
- **decision transparency** (n) - clear communication of choices
- **sustainable growth** (n) - growth that can continue safely

<summary><strong>2) Grammar & Useful Patterns (B2)</strong></summary>

- **Balanced argument language**  
  Moving fast can create opportunity, but it can also create avoidable harm.

- **Conditionals for consequences**  
  If teams ignore quality too long, customer trust drops.

- **Concession language**  
  Although speed is important, some checks are non-negotiable.

- **Recommendation language**  
  We should define clear risk thresholds before release.

- **Past lesson language**  
  We shipped too fast once and spent weeks fixing regressions.

- **Value statement language**  
  I support ambition, but not at any cost.

<summary><strong>4) Typical Dialogues</strong></summary>

### Dialogue 1 - Move Fast Debate

**Interviewer:** Is "move fast and break things" still valid?

**You:**  
It can work in early experiments, but not for critical user flows like payments or security. We once shipped too quickly and caused many regressions.

We launched faster, but spent two weeks on hotfixes. That was not real speed.

### Dialogue 2 - Ambition vs Responsibility

**Interviewer:** How do you stay ambitious without being reckless?

**You:**  
I support fast progress, but I keep non-negotiable checks: tests, rollback plan, and incident monitoring.

This adds some time, but it protects user trust and reduces long-term cost.

### Dialogue 3 - Leadership Pressure

**Interviewer:** What if leadership pushes for unsafe deadlines?

**You:**  
I explain risk in business terms: possible downtime, support cost, and customer impact. Then I propose a safer scope option.

I do not block progress, but I avoid decisions that can seriously harm users.

<summary><strong>5) Reading Text</strong></summary>

Tech culture often celebrates speed, but speed without control can become expensive. Teams may launch quickly and still lose time later in bug fixing, customer support, and trust recovery.

Ambition is valuable when paired with responsibility. Good teams move fast where risk is low and slow down where risk is high. Clear release gates and transparent communication help maintain this balance.

In interviews, avoid extreme answers. Show that you understand both growth pressure and ethical limits.

<summary><strong>6) List of Questions + Ideas</strong></summary>

### Core Questions (must-practice)
1. What does "move fast" mean in your current context?
2. When does fast execution become reckless?
3. How do you decide which risks are acceptable?
4. How do you protect quality under growth pressure?

### High-Value Case Questions
5. Share a case where moving fast caused long-term problems.
6. Tell me about a time you pushed back on an unsafe timeline.
7. Describe a release where you balanced speed and responsibility well.

### Critical Discussion Questions
8. Is "move fast and break things" a valid philosophy today?
9. Should startup teams accept more ethical risk than large companies?
10. Who should define risk boundaries: engineering or leadership?

**Tips for speaking practice:**
- Use one real trade-off example.
- Include both short-term and long-term effects.
- Stay balanced, not extreme.
