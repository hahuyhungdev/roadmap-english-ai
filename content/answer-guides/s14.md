### 1. How do you present yourself online without exaggerating your actual level?

**Common ideas:** describe real scope, use specific examples, avoid senior-sounding claims without proof.
**Useful language:** "actual scope", "I can support this claim", "honest but confident", "not inflated."
**Pattern to apply:** claim -> evidence -> limit.

**Version 1 (Clear B2):**
I present myself by staying close to what I have actually done. If I owned part of a feature, I say that instead of pretending I owned the whole product. Honest scope still sounds professional when the examples are clear.

**Version 2 (Real situation):**
Online profiles often reward big words, but I try to use language I can defend in a real conversation. For example, I would rather say "improved release communication" than "led strategic transformation" if the first sentence is more accurate.

**Version 3 (2026 context):**
In 2026, it is very easy to use AI to make a profile sound senior and polished. I think the key is to edit for clarity, not inflation. If AI helps me write better, the final profile still has to match my real experience.

### 6. When does curated identity become fake identity?

**Common ideas:** selection is normal; distortion starts when claims hide reality or create false level.
**Useful language:** "selective truth", "strategic distortion", "gap between image and reality", "inflated claim."
**Pattern to apply:** normal curation -> crossing line -> consequence.

**Version 1 (Clear B2):**
Curated identity becomes fake when it stops organizing the truth and starts changing it. Everyone chooses what to show online, but the selection should not create a false level or false story.

**Version 2 (Real situation):**
For example, it is normal to highlight your best project. It becomes fake if you describe yourself as the main owner when your role was small. The issue is not polish; the issue is misleading scope.

**Version 3 (2026 context):**
AI makes this line easier to cross because a person can sound expert before they can explain the work. Curated identity becomes fake when the language needs protection from follow-up questions. If the truth cannot support the image, the image is too inflated.

### 7. How should personal branding differ between beginner and experienced engineers?

**Common ideas:** beginners show learning, effort, real projects; experienced engineers show outcomes, judgment, influence.
**Useful language:** "career stage", "proof-based", "match real scope", "credibility compounds."
**Pattern to apply:** beginner brand -> experienced brand -> shared principle.

**Version 1 (Clear B2):**
Beginner engineers should focus on learning, small projects, and consistent progress. Experienced engineers can focus more on outcomes, decision quality, and influence. In both cases, the brand should match the real scope.

**Version 2 (Real situation):**
A beginner does not need to copy a senior engineer's tone. It is more credible to say what they are learning and show proof of effort. Senior language without senior evidence creates pressure and reduces trust.

**Version 3 (2026 context):**
In 2026, beginners can build polished portfolios quickly with AI, but they still need to show understanding. Experienced engineers also need to show judgment, not only years of experience. The common rule is simple: make the brand proof-based.
